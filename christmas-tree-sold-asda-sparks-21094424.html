Christmas tree sold at ASDA 'sparks toxic fears' among cat and dog owners

 Posted on March 18, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  400 words  |  Aldo Pusey

A Christmas tree sold at ASDA has sparked fears among cat owners after a feline reportedly became sick from the fake snow.

The alert was shared to Facebook page Reduce Your Supermarket Spend earlier this week, after initially being posted in 2016.

In the post, a cat owner said her pet Bambi had to be taken to the vets because she "couldn't walk" and was "dribbling at the mouth" after licking the fake snow from the tree she purchased.

Despite the health worry being shared almost three years ago - Asda still sell the tree online and in store for £35.

The post has since amassed 26,000 shares and 1,800 likes and 4,500 comments from worried consumers.

As the Christmas tree is still on sale, Daily Star Online contacted Asda about the concerns.

The high street giant said all products are tested to legal standards and has urged customers to contact the retailer if they experience any cause for concern.

An Asda spokesman said: “All of our products are tested to the legal standards and whilst our Christmas tree range is entirely non-toxic.

“If any customer has a safety concern about any product we would welcome them to contact our customer service team.”

15 Virtual Christmas Party Games For 2023 Easy Virtual Zoom Games

 Posted on April 6, 2024  |  8 minutes  |  1543 words  |  Martina Birk

From cocktail nights to Secret Santa, there are plenty of fun virtual Christmas games you can play with loved ones this year. For whatever reason you might not be meeting with family or loved ones, you can still gather virtually using video conferencing apps such as Zoom, Microsoft Teams and Skype.

To play, all you will need is a stable internet connection, access to your video app (you can download Zoom here) and a festive tipple to truly brighten the mood. Looking for some easy virtual games to play? Take a look at some of our favourites below...

1. Virtual Christmas karaoke

Get the whole family singing on Zoom with your very own virtual Christmas karaoke. If you're not able to gather in person this year or your loved ones live across the country or globe, we're certain this alternative will work just as well.

Guaranteed to fill your evening with laughter, simply download an online karaoke player to share using your 'screen share' button. Then, press play and the lyrics will come up, giving everyone the chance to join in. Time to practice your singing skills...

2. Secret Santa

A firm favourite at Christmastime, why not play a game of Secret Santa with your loved ones? In order to successfully play the game, follow the same steps as you normally would but then exchange the gifts virtually rather than in person. Make sure you provide the correct postal address so that everyone receives their gifts on time.

Need some Secret Santa inspiration? Here's our guide on the plastic-free gifts to give.

3. Christmas cocktail night

Pour yourself a festive tipple and have a well-deserved catch-up with a friend. It might not be the same as drinking at your favourite bar, but it's a surefire way to keep the spirits high. If you're feeling adventurous, why not make cocktails together over Zoom? Simply pick up the ingredients beforehand and whip them up together virtually.

4. Online Bingo

Bingo night just got festive. Simply download the free-to-use website Bingo Maker and create a virtual game with as many players as you want. It's a great way to ease the winter blues and spread some Christmas cheer.

5. Guess Who?

Fun for all ages, this game works by assigning each player a character. You can either choose famous faces, fictional characters or even people in your friendship circle. Once everyone has been given a character, get the other players to ask a series of "yes" and "no" questions until someone guesses correctly. It's certain to have everyone giggling.

This Christmas classic might work easier in real life, but that's not to say it can't be a huge success on Zoom too. 7. Virtual scavenger hunt

This is a fun one which will have everyone on their feet. Get one person to put together a list of common household items (like a stuffed animal, a flower, a family photo, batteries or something that starts with a specific letter) â€“ plus a few random items â€“ and send the list a minute or so before the game is set to begin. Then give everyone a time (no more than five minutes) to find as many items on the list as they can in their own home.

8. A Christmas quiz

What's Christmas without a good family quiz? Assign someone to create questions, pop them into rounds and then arrange a date for the quiz. Why not make it personal by popping in some funny pictures of those playing?

9. Virtual chocolate making night

Get hands-on this festive season with a fun virtual chocolate making team challenge. You can either hire a chef to guide you through the steps or simply find a step-by-step recipe for your friends to follow on the night. Websites such as White Rhino send you all you need to make delicious chocolate, as well as an expert. If it's not quite what you're after, there's also the option to make sushi virtually, too.

Edwin Tan//Getty Images

10. Virtual cracker making workshop

Wanting to get a little more creative? Why not make your own eco-friendly Christmas crackers virtually and then gift them to each other? Make sure everyone has the right supplies before you begin. You can either make them using recycled card or fabric cloth, depending on what you prefer.

11. Christmas costume competition

This is a fun one! All you need to do is get everyone on the call to dress up in their favourite festive costume and get someone else to rate the outfits. Make sure you provide a prize for the winner.

12. Never Have I Ever

This game is a classic ice-breaker and works well with a large group of people. To play, have participants take turns saying statements that start with "Never Have I Ever". Those who are guilty of the act must raise a hand, lower a finger, or keep their cameras on while other players turn theirs off. It's a great way to share fun experiences.

13. Two Truths, One Lie

To play this game, one person has to give three statements about themselves to the rest of the group. One of the statements will be a lie (such as 'I was born in New York') and the other team members have to guess which one is true â€” and which is a fib. You then go around the group so everyone has a turn.

LeoPatrizi//Getty Images

14. Online talent show

Add some variety to the virtual party with an online talent show. Give everyone a few days or even a week or so to prepare and see what everyone brings to the screen. From singing and dancing to magic, comedy, musical instruments and more, you can either come to a consensus on who was the most entertaining or appoint a judge.

15. Virtual escape room

Whether you've been to a physical escape room before or not, this will have everyone working together to use their problem-solving skills to break out of the virtual room before the timer runs out. There is a whole host of different themes to choose from, so you can cater to what will work best for you and your friends or family.

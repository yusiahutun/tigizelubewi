Several Kenyan tycoons have lost their lives in recent years, leaving behind multi-billion empires.

Most of them wrote wills stating how they wanted their wealth to be distributed among their children.

One of the tycoons who died and whose wealth was contested is Gerishon Kirima.

How Kirima's wealth was distributed

He died in 2010, leaving behind an estate worth over KSh 2 billion. After succession wars, Daily Nation reported that in 2020, family members finally agreed on how to share his property after a deal brokered by the High Court.

All 15 members of the Kirima family were named as beneficiaries. Under the agreement, the former Starehe MP's children were granted KSh 2 million each.

What did Gerishon Kirima own?

Parcels of land

The ex-lawmaker owned several parcels of land in Nairobi county. Among them were pieces in Kariobangi and Njiru, which were sub-divided and shared equally among family members.

Kirima also owned over 20 acres of land in Kisaju, Kajiado county. The property was distributed to all beneficiaries.

It also agreed that the land occupied by squatters would be shared equally once the court resolved the case.

On Tuesday, October 24, TUKO.co.ke reported the High Court determined that about 1000 acres of land in Njiru belong to Kirima's family.

The verdict by the High Court was a victory for Kirima's two widows, Teresia Wairimu and Anne Wangare, but a big blow to thousands of Kenyans who had bought the land and even established expensive houses.

Justice Samson Okong'o Odhiambo of the Environment and Lands Court in Kisumu issued a verdict stating that the settlers had failed to prove that they were allowed to occupy the land of the former legislator.

Kirima's Kitisuru mansion

Kirima lived in a posh mansion in the Kitisuru suburb, one of the most expensive estates to reside in Nairobi.

The house is named K&S, standing for Kirima's and Son's, a company that managed his multi-million estate.

He previously lived in Kinangop.

Rwathia Distributors

Kirima and his childhood friend Gerald Gikonyo started life as hawkers in Rwathia village, Kangema.

The duo hawked vegetables, sold charcoal and wattle barks to make ends meet before venturing into the hotel business.

According to Career Point Kenya, the duo once employed former Equity Bank chairman Peter Munga in one of their established joints.

They later co-founded Rwathia Distributors, a beer and spirits distributor for East African Breweries Ltd (EABL) and UDV Kenya Limited.

Rwathia started operations in 1964 but was incorporated in 1969 as a limited company.

Under the company, Kirima and Gikonyo purchased commercial and residential buildings in Nairobi.

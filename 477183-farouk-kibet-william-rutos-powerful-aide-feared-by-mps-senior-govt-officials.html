Most presidents have a chief political adviser, and this person is commonly portrayed as a sinister Svengali character.

Such characters wield unaccountable power behind the scenes as they whisper into the President's ear.

Farouk Kibet, President William Ruto's personal aide, is emerging as the most powerful person in the State House.

Thanks giving ceremony

On Sunday, October 9, while Ruto was away in Kampala for Uganda's 60th independence anniversary celebrations, Farouk led Kenya Kwanza troops to Uasin Gishu for a thanksgiving ceremony.

The event in Koilel, Ainabkoi, was attended by Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua, lifting the lid on Farouk's rising popularity in the new government.

Gachagua was accompanied by a huge entourage, mostly of influential men and women who are set to call the shots in the Kenya Kwanza administration for the next five years.

The calibre of politicians attending Koilel Secondary School's function showed how highly Farouk is regarded in the Kenya Kwanza administration.

Besides Gachagua, National Assembly Speaker Moses Wetangula, House Majority Leader Kimani Ichung'wah and senior political figures were present.

Farouk rose from nowhere to the inner circles of power. His shady character and operations have left people wagging their tongues.

In previous years, Farouk has been considered the point man and top aide to Ruto.

His role in Ruto's administration

Farouk is considered the one who determines the fate of those seeking to see the President either at his office or elsewhere.

The man from Turbo in Uasin Gishu is a smooth operator who always keeps a watchful eye, taking every detail for subsequent review at the President's functions.

He mentally notes who is who and who says what. Sometimes, he spots fellow power brokers in the crowd for future engagement.

He was among people whose names featured publicly over allegations of receiving money from a suspect in the National Youth Service (NYS) KSh791 million scandal.

Farouk was also mentioned by the former International Criminal Court (ICC) prosecutor, Fatou Bensouda, for alleged involvement in the 2007 post-election violence.

Bensouda claimed that Farouk stormed the Eldoret Police Station at the height of the violence.

She also accused him of tampering with ICC witnesses in the debacle that was the case against the President at The Hague.

Scanty information on Farouk's academic background

Farouk's academic background is shrouded in mystery. He is said to have attended Chepkemel Primary School, but few can pinpoint which high school he attended.

Those who know the man from Kapkechui village in Chepsaita, on the border of Uasin Gishu and Kakamega counties, said he used to roam the streets of Eldoret town in the early 1990s, engaging KANU Youth Wingers in animated discussions on how to boost the then ruling party's fortunes in Uasin Gishu and his home area of Turbo.

He would later be nominated councillor in 2002 with the help of Ruto as he slowly made his way into his boss's political backroom as a grassroots organiser.

On Sunday, Gachagua, the chief guest at the ceremony, heaped praises on Farouk, saying that he played a major role in Kenya Kwanza's August 9 victory.

"Farouk is an important leader. He played a big role to ensure we won. I had even asked the President to let him be with me but he refused. So, I'm now looking for my own Farouk," said Gachagua.

Former Nairobi governor Mike Sonko who also spoke at the event, said together with Farouk, they would play the Deep State role in the Ruto government.

"I and Farouk are now the true Deep State. Anyone who touches the President will be swept aside," said Sonko.

TUKO.co.ke previously reported that Kiharu MP Ndindi Nyoro hilariously admitted Farouk wields immense power and claimed that Gachagua seeks the aide's permission to see his boss.

Kind words for Farouk Kibet

While comparing his power to that of the president, Ndindi said no one manages to see the head of state without Farouk's approval owing to his close relationship with the head of state.

In a jocular manner, the Kiharu parliamentarian, who is also emerging as Ruto's favourite,e said the DP also goes through Farouk before accessing the President.

"Farouk's position is like that of the President of Kenya. You cannot reach the President if Farouk says no! Even for this friend of mine, the DP, Farouk, has to say yes before he meets the President," Ndindi said to the amusement of the congregation.

"Mike Sonko is here without us inviting him. God is good. Let us continue praying for him," he said.

On the aforementioned date, legendary Kenyan rapper Issah Mmari Wangui, known to many as E-Sir went to be with his maker.

As earlier reported by TUKO.co.ke, the artiste died following a grisly road accident he was involved in.

E-Sir and his crew, including Nameless were on their way from Nakuru where they had a show and were heading to Nairobi when lucifer came calling.

Well, the star's death shook the nation. Many of his fans struggled to deal with the fact that their number one, rather favourite entertainer was gone.

E-Sir died but before his demise, he had left a long list of hit songs that many Kenyans still jam to, years later.

Among his hit tracks include Mos Mos, Boomba Train, Hamunitishi, Haree and a whole lot of music in his catalogue.

As the country celebrated the star's 18th death anniversary, TUKO.co.ke thought it wise to honour his memories with a social media post.

It was through the post shared on Facebook, Instagram and Twitter that it came out clearly that many people missed the rapper.

According to quite a number of Kenyans who commented on the posts, the rapper would have been be very far in his music career today were he alive today.

Some were even confident that E-Sir would have been one of Africa's giant entertainers if death did not come his way.

TUKO.co.ke had earlier reported that most of E-Sir's friends such as Nameless, Big Pin, Nonini and the rest visited the late star's grave on Tuesday, March 16.

The group has been doing the same for the past 18 years as a way of honouring one of their fallen friends and cherish his memories while at it.

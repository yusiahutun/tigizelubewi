Parents left scratching their heads after a bizarre 'tubed' Australian snack appears on Bluey: 'What

 Posted on May 11, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  424 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

American parents have been left scratching their heads after encountering a 'strange' snack on popular Australian kids show Bluey.

Emily, from Michigan, was bewildered when she saw multi-coloured tubes being labelled 'sherbet' by Bluey and Bingo.

US natives know sherbet as a frozen dessert made with fruit juice and cream - which is a far cry from the 'sticks' Emily was faced with on television.

'They're calling this 'sherbet', but why is it in a tube? It looks like a giant pixie stick to me,' she shared in a post. 'In the US, sherbet is like ice cream, so is it something else in Australia?'

It was soon revealed that Australian sherbet is a lolly straw full of icing sugar mixed with bicarbonate soda and citric acid - a popular sweet treat that most Aussie kids enjoy.

In America, the 'pixie sticks' Emily mentioned are a similar snack, but the only difference is that it isn't 'fizzy' on the tongue.

Foodies also often confuse American sherbet with sorbet - the latter of which is dairy-free.

Bluey is one of the top kids shows in America, and Emily wasn't the only one confused by the Aussie dessert.

'What is Australian sherbet?' another parent asked on social media. 'I'm so confused.'

What is 'sherbet' in Australia? 

Australian sherbet is a mixture of citric acid and powdered sugar that is available in straws.

It is a tingly dessert most commonly found at corner stores and in various show bags.

However, American sherbet is a frozen dessert made with fruit juice and cream, which is similar to ice cream but contains less dairy.

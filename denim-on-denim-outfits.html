5 Easy Denim-On-Denim Outfits to Revamp Your Wardrobe

 Posted on August 28, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  461 words  |  Larita Shotwell

When the outfit goal is fun and fashion-forward, nothing beats a good denim-on-denim look. Beloved by some of our favorite celebrities, like Meghan Markle, Rihanna, and Kendall Jenner, this styling solution always makes a statement. Although it may seem complicated to pull off a "Canadian tuxedo" at first, it's fairly easy to create your own version. To begin, all you really need is a pair of jeans and something of the same material to wear up top, like a denim jacket or chambray shirt.

Still, it is possible to take your denim-on-denim outfit beyond a pair of flares or mom jeans. You can opt for a denim dress or dungarees, or test the waters like Dua Lipa with slip-on denim shoes. Since the washes don't need to match, the options are truly endless. 

However, if you're still searching for some ideas for how to wear denim-on-denim, take a look at the photos and tips ahead.

Begin with the Basics

Instead of pairing your jeans with a T-shirt or sweater, opt for a chambray button-down, or simply layer a denim jacket over whatever top you have for a bold look that feels less of a commitment (you can always ditch the layer later).

Think About the Details

If you're hoping to add a bit of flair to your denim-on-denim outfit, look for pieces with embellishments, such as jean jackets with beading, fringe, or embroidery. It's still the classic styling you love, just with a bit more spice.

Play Around with Different Washes

Mixing washes can be intimidating, but the real secret is that anything goes — light and dark denim, black jeans, acid wash, etc. If you feel something is missing, use playful accessories like belts, necklaces, or a printed top to break up this duo. Plus, since this ensemble is already a statement, there's no need to fuss around with footwear. Solid booties or sneakers will provide balance and complete the look.

Get Dressy

Denim dresses are the best of both worlds: casual enough for everyday wear, yet depending on design, can be sleek and elevated. While they make a great one-and-done piece, layering a denim jacket over your look, adding denim boots, or slipping on a denim hat will quickly take things to the next level.

Overhaul Your Overalls

Dungarees used to have a reputation for being juvenile, but it's possible to amp up your average pair with other jean pieces. Layer a denim duster jacket over your outfit for a Y2K-approved spin, or replace the shirt underneath with a chambray alternative. It's an unexpected way to wear a classic piece.

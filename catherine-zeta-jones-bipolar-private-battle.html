    However, following her time in the clinic, the actress has been open about her reluctance to discuss her mental health. She told magazine: "I'm not the kind of person who likes to shout out my personal issues from the rooftops but, with my bipolar becoming public, I hope fellow sufferers will know it's controllable".

    She explained once more to that her mental health wasn't something she hoped would become public. "I never wanted to be as open about it as I was," she told the newspaper. "I have a British stiff-upper-lip mentality –- it wasn't something I wanted to shout from the rooftops."

The teenager who filmed the alleged sexual assault of a drunken 16-year-old at the centre of the Ohio rape case was today dramatically forced to testify by a judge after he sought to invoke the 5th Amendment.

In an explosive exchange during the third day of the trial of two Steubenville footballers, the witness - who was key to the case being brought in the first place - had his bid to exercise his right denied.

He went on to describe how he filmed - and then deleted from his iPhone - footage of one fo the accused, Trent Mays, digitally penetrating her in the back of a car, how Mays put his penis in the mouth of the teenage girl and how he viewed the notorious picture of her being carried by her arms and legs as a 'bad joke'.

Moment of truth: Trent Mays on the day he and friend Ma'lik Richmond were convicted of raping, degrading and humiliating their 16-year-old victim. Mays could be free next year

The witness told the court that he did not see the alleged victim speak or move while Mays touched her intimately

Judge Lipps said that the testimony would ‘further the administration of justice (therefore) you are hereby ordered and compelled to answer questions and produce testimony.’

Yet in a move that will infuriate many, the assured the 17-year-old, a close friend of Mays, 17, and his co-accused Ma’lik Richmond, 16, that the immunity he had been granted at the probable cause hearing last October continued to protect him from prosecution in the State of Ohio.

Last September the state’s Attorney General issued three almost identical letters promising immunity to key witnesses if they testified. 

The State has received a petition with close to 90,000 signatures calling from the prosecution of this witness.

His house was the site of the incident on the August night described by the prosecution as one of ‘degradation, humiliation and violation,’ of a 16-year-old girl too drunk to know what was happening.

He was repeatedly asked to speak up during close to two hours of testimony in which he admitted filming Mays digitally penetrating the unresponsive girl in the back of a car.

Escape: The teenage witness deleted the footage the morning after and because of that he has dodged charges himself

He only escaped charges for doing this because the file was deleted and cannot be technically retrieved from the iPhone he used.

He also revealed that he had witnessed Mays trying to insert his penis into her partially opened mouth as she lay on the floor of his basement.

He also sent a text to Mays at 2.38am on August 12 which read: 'Seriously dude don’t f*** rape her.’

With his attorney at his side the youth, who has known both accused for several years through wrestling and football, told how the alleged victim ‘threw up between her legs’ on the street before ‘being assisted’ to his car following the second of three parties that night.

He himself was too drunk to drive so joined the alleged victim who sat behind the driver and Mays, seated in the middle, in the rear. Richmond sat in the front passenger seat.

He said: ‘We went to my house. Her shirt was off and Trent had started f****ring her and that’s when I took my phone out and started recording.’

By this stage in the night there were only five people present: the two accused, three male friends including the witness and the alleged victim.

They looked through the messages on her phone – a fact used by the defense to suggest the alleged victim was coherent as she managed to give them her passcode.

The prosecution allege the opposite: What teenage girl would sit idly by while five boys scrolled through their most intimate thoughts and words?

The witness left his house to visit an ex girlfriend around 2.30am. He sent that text telling Mays not to rape her at 2.38am. ‘Just about the time when it was all happening,' said Brian Derrant for the prosecution. 

The next morning he said he recalled looking at his phone, seeing the footage from the car the night before and deleting it.

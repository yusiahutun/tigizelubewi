The founding year of RSC Anderlecht

RSC Anderlecht, also known as Royal Sporting Club Anderlecht, was founded in 1908 in Anderlecht, Brussels, Belgium. It has since become one of the most successful football clubs in Belgian history.

RSC Anderlecht’s iconic stadium

RSC Anderlecht plays their home matches at the Constant Vanden Stock Stadium, which has a seating capacity of over 21,000 spectators. The stadium has witnessed numerous historic moments in the club’s history.

The most successful Belgian football club

RSC Anderlecht is widely regarded as the most successful football club in Belgium. It has won 34 Belgian Pro League titles, more than any other club in the country.

European success

RSC Anderlecht has achieved significant success in European competitions. The club has won the UEFA Cup Winners’ Cup twice, in 1976 and 1978, and reached the final of the UEFA Champions League in 1983.

The Purple and Whites

RSC Anderlecht is commonly known as “The Purple and Whites” due to the club’s traditional team colors. The purple and white combination has become synonymous with the club’s identity.

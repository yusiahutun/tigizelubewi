Experts on the dangers of Ozempic and skinny injections

 Posted on March 22, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  1037 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Quick fixes for weight loss are nothing new – especially during the month of January. “From elixirs and laxatives prescribed by the Greek physician Soranus of Ephesus in the 2nd century AD, to the emergence of appetite suppressants like amphetamines in the 1930s,” we have a long and complicated relationship with diet culture, notes Peter Cobby – a fitness coach to myriad models. The latest trend? So-coined ‘skinny injections’, aka semaglutide – the active ingredient in branded medications such as Ozempic and Wegovy. With Google searches for the drugs soaring this new year, and videos tagged '#ozempic' amassing three billion views on TikTok to date, it’s positioned by many as a golden ticket to weight loss – but there’s much more to semaglutide than that.

“Semaglutide comes in the form of a weekly injection and is used to help people with high BMIs lose weight,” Dr Mitra Dutt from LloydsPharmacy Online Doctor tells Bazaar. “It works by suppressing the appetite and regulating food intake. Although originally used as a diabetes medication, semaglutide is now a licensed weight-loss treatment, effective alongside a healthy, reduced-calorie diet and increased physical activity.” (Note that Ozempic is still only indicated for the treatment of adults with insufficiently controlled type 2 diabetes mellitus, and that use of it as a weight-management medication is considered 'off-label'.)

Indeed, “semaglutide should not be mistaken for a magic weight-loss medication,” Dr Dutt says – as alone it is not necessarily effective. Nor should it be misused by those who aren’t suitable candidates.

“Semaglutide should not be mistaken for a magic weight-loss medication”

“Semaglutide is not for everyone; usually, you must have a BMI of 30 or above,” and therefore considered obese, according to the oft-controversial body mass index measurement that uses your height and weight to work out if you’re considered healthy. “However, if you have a weight-related health condition, such as asthma or osteoarthritis, the treatment may be recommended if you have a BMI over 27,” she continues.

“You should also avoid semaglutide if you have a history of certain medical conditions such as heart failure,” the doctor adds. “For this reason, it’s paramount you use a safe medicated weight-loss prescriber like LloydsPharmacy Online Doctor who will assess your medical history to ensure the treatment is suitable for you and will offer ongoing support and monitoring.”

Cobby feels that the ease and accessibility of drugs like Ozempic and Wegovy aligns with its misuse. “With online pharmacies providing 24/7 access via short questionnaires that can see the drug delivered direct to your door, it’s use is now very much mainstream,” he says, offering that when experimenting with obtaining the drug himself, “the ease I was able to order was of concern”. But the fashion industry has a part to play in its desirability, he admits. “The spring/summer fashion shows in 2023 saw the re-emergence of a ‘super skinny’ aesthetic. And while there was backlash in the media, this just drew more attention to the shows.” However, he feels the lure of weight loss is always there, despite peaking at certain times for certain people. “Around 90 per cent of my clientele come to me with the same request,” he reveals.

Dr Dutt is keen to point out how users may react to semaglutide. “Common side effects include nausea, fatigue and indigestion. You may also experience constipation, diarrhoea, vomiting and increased burping.

"It’s easy to see why customers may become desensitised to treatments offered via injections"

“A rare but serious side effect can be acute pancreatitis or inflammation of the pancreas. Usually, cases of acute pancreatitis improve quickly but a small proportion of cases are severe. This is why it’s important to let your doctor know if you experience side effects like abdominal pain or vomiting.”

There are many more risks associated with misuse of semaglutide, “which can have serious consequence,” she adds. “These include serious dehydration, leading to kidney damage and significant hair loss.”

Worryingly, “there have also been reports of semaglutide supply issues leading to people illegally seeking counterfeit versions of the drug”, the doctor reveals. Those who are trying to gain access to semaglutide without a prescription will have likely encountered copycat versions too – sometimes unknowingly. “This has been linked with hospitalisations as some patients experience dangerously low sugar levels, causing seizures,” Dr Dutt says.

According to a recent report, the Medicines and Healthcare products Regulatory Agency (MHRA) has seized 369 potentially fake Ozempic pens since January 2023, with Dr Alison Cave, MHRA chief safety officer “advising all members of the public not to use any pre-filled weight-loss pens they may have bought online and instead to report it to us so that we can investigate and take any necessary action”.

"It’s human nature to find a quicker way to do something, but our health shouldn't be addressed with speed"

It speaks to the culture around the normalisation of needles for cosmetic gains, says BABTAC chief executive and chair Lesley Blair MBE. “As consumer interest has shifted to become more open-minded to non-surgical procedures and injectables, it’s easy to see why customers may become desensitised to treatments offered via injections.” However, when doing so, it is paramount to question whether the provider is a medical professional, or even legally allowed to prescribe the drug, she adds. Not to mention your suitability for use.

As Dr Dutt puts it: “If you want to explore how semaglutide could aid your weight-loss journey, it’s incredibly important that you seek a reputable provider whose clinicians adhere to safeguarding checks, such as BMI checks and medical history assessments.”

And be mindful of diet culture influencing your motives. “It’s within our nature to try and find a quicker way to do something, but our health is not something that I feel needs to be addressed with speed,” Cobby shares. “If you are part of the minority that could actually benefit from the use of one of these drugs, then by all means talk to your doctor or healthcare specialist about it. For everyone else looking to achieve a healthy weight, start creating some lasting habits that are in line with your goals. Small, compounding steps in the right direction will lead to much longer-lasting results, and a good quality of life.”

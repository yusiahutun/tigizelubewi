What is Gua Sha, And What Are Its Benefits?

Gua Sha is an ancient Chinese medicinal practice that involves a smooth-edged tool to scrape skin. It's a massage technique that increases blood circulation in the affected area to provide various health benefits. The practice can be performed on different body parts, such as the face, neck, legs, arms, or back. When engaging in gua sha for the facial skin, the masseur uses a gua sha tool, typically carved out of jade or rose quartz, to give optimum pressure by long and downward strokes to increase blood flow in the facial muscles. Although you may do this treatment at home, it's better to consult a professional in case of existing health issues. 

Coming to its benefits, the process improves overall skin health. It soothes inflammation, increases lymphatic drainage, stimulates circulation, and releases skin tension to relieve pain. Moreover, it helps sculpt your facial contours and delays premature aging so you can celebrate youthfulness anytime. 

Using a good face oil for gua sha enhances the process efficacy without irritation. Plus, it lubricates the skin so the tool can glide smoothly. The oil penetrates the skin layers to boost moisture and retain water content to keep the skin healthy and plump.

    Cold Pressed v/s Heat Pressed Oils 

    When it comes to oils, you may encounter these two buzzing words on the product labels. So here we have done the complete breakdown to help you understand the difference. 

    Cold-pressed oils are extracted at low temperatures through mechanical methods like pressing. This preserves the natural nutritional value and potency. Contrastingly, hot-pressed oils are manufactured at higher temperatures. Plus, they are treated with chemicals to maintain the acidity levels. Hence, most essential nutrients are destroyed with less or no nutritional value. Therefore, cold-pressed oils are the clear winner you can go with. 

    Consistency 

    The oil's consistency is a matter of personal choice. Some like a thicker consistency, while others prefer a thin texture. However, it's better to ensure the oil doesn't sit on the surface and absorbs well without leaving residue or feeling heavy.

    How to Apply the Best Face Oil for Gua Sha?

    Here are the steps you can follow to use the gua sha face oil correctly. 

  • Start with a thorough and gentle cleanse. Before gliding your gua sha stone, it's crucial to remove dead skin cells and other impurities for better oil absorption. 
  • Prime your skin with a lightweight facial essence formula to add moisture and smoothen the surface. 
  • Apply 2-3 drops of your favorite gua sha oil and glide the tool gently in circular motions or straight strokes. 
  • Put the amount of pressure you are comfortable with. 
  • Complete your routine by applying a mild moisturizer to lock in moisture.

    FAQ's

    Is it important to use a face oil for gua sha?

    Applying face oil helps glide the stone carefully and accurately. This ensures that the gua sha tool goes smoothly without tugging. Plus, the oil absorbs deep into the skin to nourish and hydrate it for a healthy glow.

    What should I avoid after gua sha treatment?

    After a gua sha treatment, you must avoid exposure to cold. Avoid sitting near air conditioners or fans as these may constrict the vessels, leading to low blood circulation over the area. Plus, you should refrain from applying too much pressure to prevent redness.

    How long should I go for gua sha?

    Typically, dermatologists recommend going for the process three to four times a week with a session of three to five minutes each.

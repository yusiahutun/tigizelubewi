The Oldest Mosque in Sofia, Bulgaria

Banya Bashi Mosque, also known as the Sultan Bayezid II Mosque, is the oldest functioning mosque in Sofia, the capital city of Bulgaria. It was built during the Ottoman rule in the 16th century and still serves as an important religious site for the country’s Muslim community.

An Architectural Masterpiece

The mosque’s design is a beautiful blend of Ottoman and Byzantine architectural styles. Its distinctive minaret and elegant dome attract visitors from around the world, making it a significant landmark in Sofia.

A Unique Foundation

Banya Bashi Mosque is built on thermal springs, hence the name “Banya Bashi,” which translates to “many baths” in Turkish. The thermal water beneath the mosque is believed to have healing properties and is a popular destination for those seeking natural remedies.

Survived Earthquakes and Wars

Despite being located in an area prone to earthquakes, Banya Bashi Mosque has withstood several seismic events over the centuries. The mosque has also survived numerous wars and conflicts, bearing witness to the rich history of Bulgaria.

A Symbol of Tolerance

The mosque stands as a symbol of religious tolerance and coexistence in Sofia. It is surrounded by other places of worship, including an Orthodox church and a synagogue, serving as a testament to Bulgaria’s multicultural heritage.

Mesmerizing Interior Decorations

The interior of Banya Bashi Mosque is adorned with intricate calligraphy and beautiful geometric patterns. The serene atmosphere inside the mosque invites worshippers and visitors alike to appreciate its architectural and artistic marvels.

Open to All

Despite being a place of worship, Banya Bashi Mosque welcomes visitors of all faiths and backgrounds. It provides a peaceful sanctuary where anyone can explore the rich Islamic culture and experience the beauty of the mosque.

A Center for Community Gatherings

Banya Bashi Mosque not only serves as a place of prayer but also acts as a community center for the local Muslim population. Social events, educational activities, and religious ceremonies are organized in the mosque, fostering a sense of unity and belonging.

An Oasis of Tranquility

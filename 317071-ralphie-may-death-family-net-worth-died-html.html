Ralph Duren May was a stand-up comedian, producer, writer, and actor who made the world happier with his jokes. He is fondly remembered for easily connecting with diverse audiences and making them laugh.

Where was Ralphie May from?

Educational background

Ralphie went to Clarksville High School. After graduating high school, he moved to the Sagemont area of Houston, Texas, where he attended San Jacinto Community College South Campus.

Career

Ralphie started his stand-up comedy career in Houston, Texas. He started at The Comedy Showcase on Fuqua in Houston. He was there for a while before moving to Los Angeles to advance his career.

In 2003, he was selected to participate in the first season of Last Comic Standing. He came in second place in the competition.

After the competition, he appeared in The Wayne Brady Show, The Tonight Show with Jay Leno, and The Big Black Comedy Show. Before becoming a successful comedian, he worked as a chef at Four Seasons in Houston, NOLA’s, and Emeril Lagasse’s restaurant in New Orleans.

In 2005, he released his first comedy album titled Just Correct. He then recorded Comedy Central and Netflix specials, as listed below. He also did extensive comedy tours before his demise, and often appeared as a guest on the Opie and Anthony show on XM Satellite Radio and Rover’s Morning Glory.

How did Ralphie May die?

On Friday, 6th October 2017, a friend found Ralphie dead at a private residence in Las Vegas, Nevada. He had performed at Harrah's Las Vegas Hotel Thursday night.

Following his death, his publicist said the comedian had been battling pneumonia. He added that Ralphie had cancelled several dates over the last month to allow himself time to recover.

What was Ralphie May's cause of death?

What did Ralphie May die from? The comedian's cause of death was hypertensive cardiovascular disease. No illegal or hard substances were found in his system at the time of demise, so it was ruled a natural death.

Where is Ralphie May's grave?

The comedian was buried at Oakland Memorial Cemetery, Clarksville, Arkansas, United States of America. A memorial graveside service was held on Saturday, 4th November 2017, and Bro. Sam Hannon officiated the ceremony.

OBJ abbreviation meaning

Object Replacement Character, popularly represented as OBJ in text, is a text placeholder or unrecognized object in your Android or iOS. All the content you see on social media are objects which your device displays as emojis or characters.

The OBJ was given meaning by the Unicode system. Unicode is the standard representation of different characters, symbols, and emojis used as a reference for computers to understand each other.

What does OBJ mean in a text message?

You have probably seen an OBJ acronym while composing a text message and wondered what is going on. This normally happens when your text app fails to correctly identify a character.

This might be because you don’t have the character on your phone’s version of emojis. The OBJ text symbol will appear as a dotted square with the word OBJ inside.

In short, what OBJ means in a text message is that your text app cannot show the sent character on your device.

But what does OBJ mean in text, and what causes it to appear on your social media? Here are things that can cause the occurrence of the OBJ emoji:

Software bug

A software bug is the main reason you see an OBJ emoji on your social media, especially on Android devices. This issue can be dealt with by updating the social media app you are using as well as updating your keyboard. If that fails, you might need to update the Operating System of your device or download the font libraries.

Use of Voice-To-Text function devices

The Voice to Text function on iPhones is a favorite for iOS users to send messages, search for things, and give recognizable automated commands.

  • What does the OBJ file contain? The OBJ file contains a standard 3D image with a dotted box with OBJ in the middle that can be exported and opened by various 3D image editing programs. It is commonly visible to users on Facebook, YouTube, Snapchat, or TikTok.

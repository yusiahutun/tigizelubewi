Fast and Furious Franchise Cast and Character Guide

Spoilers follow for The Fast and the Furious franchise and especially for Fast & Furious Presents: Hobbs & Shaw.

Fast and Furious franchise has been around for 18 years, a fact celebrated by the release of DavidLeitch's Fast & Furious Presents: Hobbs & Shawlast year. After nearly $5 billion from the worldwide box office so far, the Universal Pictures property shows no signs of slowing down. And yet with nine movies and counting, including the recently released spinoff and the upcoming Fast 9, it's difficult to keep track of the myriad characters who have come and gone over the years. The core crew has stayed more or less the same, albeit with some heartbreaking losses and surprising additions, so we've put a guide together that will get you caught up with the story so far.

This cast and character guide aims to hit the major players and remind fans where they started, how they've evolved, and where they are now. Sadly, this means we won't be revisiting one-film wonders like Leon and Jesse, Suki, or Fenix Calderon in detail. However, we now know all about Charlize Theron's bad-ass villain and Helen Mirren's mysterious character in the eighth installment, along with more on Dwayne Johnson's characters backstory and family tree as well as the extended Shaw family with Jason Statham and Vanessa Kirby, so you can find out more on them below. This guide is for the tried and true on Dom's Crew, and the occasional characters who cross their path. Let's get it in gear!

Vin Diesel as Dominic Toretto

*Big thanks to the Fast and Furious wiki for their help in putting this guide together.

The Fast and the Furious - With only a handful of acting credits on his resume as of 2001, Vin Diesel became a household name when he was introduced to audiences as Dominic "Dom" Toretto. The son of a stock car racer who died on the track, Dom did a two-year stint in Lompoc Prison for assaulting the racer who clipped his father's car during a race and ultimate caused his death. Audiences meet Dom and his crew-which includes childhood sweetheart Letty Ortiz and his friends, Vince, Leon, and Jesse-as they participate in illegal street races and boost high-end electronics from tractor trailers in transit.

The plot thickens when Brian O'Conner, an undercover cop going by the name of Brian Earl Spilner infiltrates Dom's crew, earns their friendship, and even wins the heart of Dom's sister, Mia, despite Dominic's protests. Together they are (mostly) able to survive the bad blood with a rival gang led by Johnny Tran and a botched attempt at stealing one last batch of electronics before fleeing to Mexico. When Dom learns of Brian's true identity, he's surprised to find that the cop is letting him go after Tran and his henchman who have shot one of their own. Their tense friendship is put to a final test in a quarter-mile race that ends in a horrific crash that Dom barely survives; Brian gifts him the keys to his own ten-second car that he owed him, allowing him to escape the police who are soon to arrive.

2 Fast 2 Furious - Diesel has no on-screen presence, but Dom is often mentioned by other characters.

Los Bandoleros - This 2009 short film was written, directed, and produced by Diesel, who starred alongside his franchise cast members. This film sees Dom and his friends in the Dominican Republic after leaving Mexico behind. After befriending some of the locals, they plan to bust one of them out of prison before heading off to hijack a gasoline shipment; Dom intends for the gas to be distributed to the citizens. Later, he's found out be Letty, who tracked him from Mexico. The two rekindle their romance and decide to get married in a local church, vowing that they'll "ride or die" together.

Fast & Furious - After successfully stealing the gas from a tanker, the team decides to split their earnings and go their separate ways. Despite their recent marriage, Dom parts ways with Letty, but leaves his share of the money and a cross to her. Tragically, while Dom is in Panama, he's informed of Letty's death in a fatal car crash; Dom and his sister Mia suspect foul play, and he returns to Los Angeles to investigate. His search leads him to a man named David Park, the same man sought by now-FBI agent, Brian O'Conner.

Dom and Brian soon end up facing off in a Los Angeles street race to become a driver for international drug dealer Arturo Braga. Dom wins his place after forcing Brian and his car out of the race, but Brian is able to find a way onto the team as well. Soon, Dom spurns the affections of one of Braga's employees, Gisele Yashar, thinking only of Letty. He also meets one of Braga's henchmen by the name of Fenix Calderon and soon learns that he's responsible for Letty's murder.

Dom survives the race across the border and Braga's ultimate betrayal of his drivers by boosting a Hummer and $60 million worth of heroin, with only a gunshot wound for his trouble. He and Brian hide the drugs in a police impound lot in Los Angeles where they reunited with Mia. They plan to lure Braga out of hiding with the drugs in exchange for Brian's superiors granting Dom a full pardon. Unfortunately, Braga uses a decoy and escapes the trap, fleeing to Mexico and nearly killing Gisele during his flight back to Mexico.

With Gisele's assistance, they're able to apprehend Braga and drive him back to the U.S., though his men pursue them all the way. Dom also gets revenge for Letty's murder by hitting Fenix with a Camaro at high speed and pinning his body against another wrecked car. Rather than run from the police who are arriving on scene, Dom stays and awaits his fate, which is his arrest and sentencing to 25 years in Lompoc without parole, despite Brian's connections. En route to the prison, his team-including Mia and Brian-plan to break him out by preparing to crash the bus.

Fast Five - After busting Dom out of prison, he and his team become wanted felons. Dom separates from them and flees to Ecuador where he reunites with Vince in Rio. Together with Brian and Mia, they plan to boost cars from a train outside the city, though Dom does not trust Vince's new accomplices who try to call the shots. Though Mia is able to escape the inevitable betrayal (in a car that contains a much sought-after microchip), Dom and Brian are temporarily taken hostage after a death-defying plunge from a cliff into a river far beneath a bridge. They meet the man behind the heist, Hernan Reyes, and manage to escape his clutches.

Meeting up with Mia at their safe house, they soon find out they've been framed for the murder of DEA agents aboard the train. Brian blames Vince for the double-cross, but Dom sticks up for his old friend. However, Vince covertly takes the chip back to Reyes, blaming Dom for Letty's death because he never listened to Vince back in Los Angeles. When their safe house is ambushed the next day, Dom leads them away from Brian and Mia but is pursued over the village rooftops by DSS agent Luke Hobbs and ultimately saves fellow agent Elena Neves from Reyes' men.

After Dom learns that he's going to be an uncle, he then plans to steal the $100 million of laundered money from Reyes despite being hunted by the criminal and the DSS alike. Dom forms up a new team and forces Reyes to consolidate his money in one place by burning down one of his safe houses and planning to do the same to the rest of his money. (They also have a quarter-mile race for $1 million, which Dom lets Brian win as an early gift to his new nephew.) Before their plan can be put into action, Dom and Hobbs engage in a brutal hand-to-hand fight, which Dom wins but is ultimately arrested along with his team.

However, their convoy to the airport for extradition back to the U.S. is attacked by Reyes' men. During the fight, all but one of Reyes' men is killed, though Vince is mortally wounded. Hobbs and Dom now form an alliance in order to seek revenge on Reyes for the death of their friends and family. While on an insane race through the city streets with a vault full of Reyes' money being hauled behind their cars, Dom takes it upon himself to wipe out the corrupt police on his own and crashes the vault into Reyes' car. Despite his heroics, Hobbs refuses to let Dom walk away, but he does give him a 24-hour head start.

The joke's on Hobbs, however, since Dom and his crew switched vaults and now split the $100 million. Dom leaves Vince's share with his wife Rosa and son Nico, then moves to Spain's Canary Island with Mia, Brian, and Elena.

Fast & Furious 6- Dom and Brian race to a local church for the birth of Mia's son, Jack; both of them are excited to usher in a new generation of their family and they consider putting their old ways behind them. However, when Hobbs shows up asking Dom to help him track down an ex-British Special Forces soldier named Owen Shaw, in exchange for his assistance, Dom soon learns that Letty is still alive. Elena convinces Dom to help Hobbs, but he offers to do so without the rest of his team. Too bad Hobbs and the team themselves refuse to sit idly by.

Dom learns that Letty is running with Shaw's team in London and is more interested in finding her than in helping Hobbs prevent Shaw from constructing a "Nightshade" device which would disable a country's power supply and be auctioned off to the highest bidder. Dom's team, who will also earn full pardons and have their criminal records erased for their assistance, split up to stop Shaw; Dom heads to the villain's hideout with Hobbs and his team. After racing through the city, Dom is drawn off by Letty, who soon shoots him in the shoulder and immediately flees from him.

Dom follows up on a lead that suggests Letty has been competing in local street races and tracks her down, doing his best to trigger his "ride or die" wife's memories. He attempts to convince her about their shared past by noting her scars, their past races together, and their various trips around the world, before finally gifting her the cross that's long been a symbol between them. Shaw soon arrives and attempts to use Letty as an edge over Dom, but Hobbs also appears in order to back up his teammate.

When Shaw next steals a military tank in order to get his hands on a powerful CPU, Dom's team attempts to stop the tank in its tracks on the highway. This results in Dom crashing his car and launching himself across a vast canyon in order to catch Letty and prevent her from falling to her death after she was thrown from the tank. Unfortunately, during their operation, Shaw's men kidnapped Mia to use as a bargaining chip. The CPU goes with Shaw's team while Letty stays with Dom and his partners.

Racing to a nearby airstrip to prevent Shaw's escape, Dom boards the plane to save Mia, having to battle the massive Klaus in the process. After Mia is saved, Dom goes after Shaw and is able to nab the Nightshade devices before making an explosive escape by driving a Dodge Charger out of the crashing plane. Though his car flips and crashes itself, Dom emerges relatively unscathed.

The team reunites at Dom's house in Los Angeles for a barbecue where Dom and Elena amicably part ways and everyone enjoys their newfound freedom after their criminal records are expunged.

The Fast and the Furious: Tokyo Drift - Dom travels to Tokyo to bring his fallen friend Han's remains back to the U.S. after his death, but not before participating in a street race with the Drift King of Tokyo, Sean Boswell. After Dom wins, Sean gives him some items from Han's car, including a picture of Gisele and Dom's familiar cross.

Furious 7 - Dom and Letty attend Race Wars in an effort to jog her memory, but an episode causes her to have an adverse reaction. Seeking to find herself, Letty leaves Dom behind at the graveyard where she was presumably once laid to rest. Dom then reunites with Mia their family home and learns that she's pregnant again, this time with a baby girl. Their celebration is short-lived since a package on their front porch is actually a bomb which destroys the house, though Mia is protected from the explosion by Dom.

Visiting Hobbs at a hospital soon after this incident, Hobbs learns that Shaw's brother Deckard is an assassin seeking to avenge Owen's severe injuries. Dom now knows that Deckard is responsible for laying up Hobbs, killing Han, and destroying his family home. He soon gets a chance to match his racing skill against Shaw when he spots the assassin at Han's funeral. After totaling each of their cars in an unflinching game of chicken, Dom and Shaw face off in battle, though Dom brings a sledgehammer to a gun fight. Dom is saved by a team of covert ops soldiers who shoot at Shaw and force him to retreat. Their leader, a man known as Mr. Nobody, convinces Dom that they're on the same side and he can help him get Shaw.

Dom and his team, now including Letty once again, accept Mr. Nobody's offer to track down Ramsey. a hacker who has been abducted by a terrorist thanks to her involvement with developing surveillance system known as the God's Eye, in order to locate Shaw. Their plan involves parachuting down to Ramsey's location in Azerbaijan's Caucasus Mountains in their beefed-up cars. Despite the best attempts of both Shaw and the terrorist, Dom rescues Ramsey and they survive the chase. Unfortunately, Ramsey has sold the God's Eye to a prince in Abu Dhabi, so they must go undercover to infiltrate his lavish party. Dom and Brian are able to escape the prince's guards by driving a car from one luxury tower into the next (into the next...), ripping out the God's Eye device before the car ultimately crashes to the ground far below.

Though they're now able to track down Shaw, Dom must save a wounded Mr. Nobody during a botched assault in which the God's Eye device falls into the hands of the terrorists. Dom plans to deal with Shaw back in Los Angeles, but gives Letty his necklace and promises to return for it; he then goes back to his old garage, uncovers the famous 1970 Dodge Charger, and makes his preparations. Dom and Shaw have a final showdown in a parking lot where, despite drawing a shotgun on him, Dom engages in down and dirty street fight with the assassin. Dom is able to get the upper hand by stomping on a section of the parking lot which collapses beneath Shaw's feet. With Shaw defeated, Dom also assists in the takedown of a military helicopter piloted by the terrorist by jumping a car off the parking garage ramp and snagging a bag of grenades on the helicopter's skids before crashing the car below.

Letty finds Dom and cradles his unconscious body, confessing that her memories have returned, to which he responds, "It's about time." Having saved the day, Dom and the team relax on a beach; he becomes emotional watching Brian with his family, knowing that his old friend needs to retire from this dangerous life. Dom and Brian bid each other farewell as they drive off in separate directions.

The Fate of the Furious - While we previously thought Dom would be returning to face off against Cipher (Charlize Theron), the greatest threat the team has seen yet, it's now quite clear that these two are actually teaming up! This breaks up the family unit that's been the core of the franchise from the beginning. But all is not as it seems.

Dom is on his honeymoon with Letty in Cuba, where he still finds time to do a little streetracing in order to help out his cousin Marcos, when he's interrupted by Cipher. She has something in her possession that forces Dom to work for her and seemingly betray his friends and all-important family. It turns out that the only thing that would make Dom turn heel is the fact that Elena was being held hostage ... along with their infant son.

During a hijacking of a powerful EMP device in Berlin by Dom's team, he goes rogue and takes the device on Cipher's orders. Constantly being tested by the cyberterrorist and kept at arm's length from Elena and his son, Dom is then forced to steal codes to Russian nuclear missiles, despite his former team's best efforts to stop him. However, he hesitates to kill Letty during the heist; Cipher punishes him by having her henchman Rhodes execute Elena.

While working out a plan on the sly to rescue his infant son, Dom heads to Russia in order to sabotage a nuclear submarine and give Cipher access to the nukes. However, once Dom's son is in safe hands, Toretto gets revenge on both Rhodes and Cipher, and shows his true colors by rallying to his teammates' side to save the day. In classic Fast and the Furious fashion, they're all reunited in a rooftop barbecue at the movie's end, during which Dom introduces his son, Brian.

Paul Walker as Brian O'Conner

The Fast and the Furious - Though no one has appeared in every single Fast and Furious film, Diesel and Paul Walker have come the closest. Tragically, Walker lost his life in a car accident that hit too close to home for the friends, family, and fandom who had come to know him as Brian O'Conner from the Universal Pictures franchise. Furious 7 saw a fitting send-off for the late Walker and his character, one which took a look back at his most exciting and emotional moments from the franchise.

Brian O'Conner was introduced to audiences as an L.A.P.D. officer hoping to make detective, but his backstory has been filled in a little more over the years. He grew up in Barstow and was childhood friends with fellow trouble-maker Roman Pearce; he never knew his father and was raised by his mother. Brian's involvement with law enforcement cost him his friendship with Roman, but an undercover assignment investigating a string of highway hijackings led to new friendships with a number of low-level criminals in Dominic Toretto's crew.

Brian's cover as an employee for auto parts shop The Racer's Edge brings him into proximity with Toretto's Market & Cafe, along with his love interest Mia, and the lead suspect in his case, Dom Toretto. A dust-up between Brian and Dom's friend Vince over continued flirtation with Mia puts Brian squarely on Dom's radar, but his police connections keeps his cover intact. It also gives him access to plenty of nitrous oxide which he uses to participate in illegal street races. On his first outing, he wagers his pink slips against other racers and loses quite badly to Dom, who takes his law enforcement-sanctioned street rod. Adding insult to injury, the car is soon forfeit due to Brian and Dom wandering into rival Johnny Tran's territory.

As Brian is pulled into Dom's inner circle, the cops make a show of arresting him in order to be brought up to speed with his new intel. Due to the burgeoning friendship with Dom and the budding romance with Mia, Brian opts to investigate Tran's crew instead of Dom's. Their bonds grow closer as they work on restoring a new car for Brian to race, something Special Agent Bilkins and Sergeant Tanner keep an eye on as they're concerned that their undercover agent is "going native." In order to keep him on the straight and narrow, they show Brian the file on Kenny Linder, a racer Dom nearly beat to death years earlier.

But after hearing Dom's side of the story, and after pressure from his superiors, Brian opts to pull the trigger on arresting Tran's crew for the heists instead of seeing the real criminals right in front of him. When that operation goes poorly, Brian's last chance to get in on Dom's side business is to win at Race Wars using his new car. Unfortunately, tensions between rival gangs spill out during the race event, and Brian is forced to expose his cover to Mia. His betrayal puts their relationship in jeopardy, but she informs him of Dom's plans so that he can hopefully keep the heist team from getting killed by the armed truck driver they're about to hijack. Brian is able to intercept the failed robbery and save Vince from certain death, but in doing so he reveals his true identity to Dom and his crew, forming a rift between them.

However, he can't save Dom's friend Jesse, who is gunned down by Lance, one of Tran's crew. Brian pursues the hitman in a high-speed chase and eventually shoots Tran to death, but is saved from being shot himself when Dom takes out Lance. Brian and Dom's relationship is put to the ultimate test when a quarter-mile race against an oncoming train leaves Dom injured and his car destroyed; Brian gifts Dom the ten-second car he owed him and gives him a free pass from the cops ... this time.

Turbo-Charged Prelude - If you were left wondering what happened to Brian after his decision to shirk responsibility and let a criminal go free, you should check out this 2003 short film. It finds Brian packing up his belongings, except for his police badge, in order to drive across the American Southwest in his Mitsubishi 3000GT. Along the way, he meets a mysterious girl who helps him avoid the local and federal authorities who have organized a manhunt to find him. He eventually has to ditch the Mitsubishi and opts for a Nissan Skyline GT-R R34 instead, upgrading it as he goes with his earnings from various street races. After opting out of a race against motorcyclists, Brian decides to head towards Miami instead of New York City. It's there that he befriends Tej Parker and starts working at the former street racer's garage, earning the nickname "Bullitt" as he builds his own reputation.

2 Fast 2 Furious - It's in Miami that Brian ends up winning a quarter-mile race as a last-minute racer in a four-man competition. His victory is short-lived however since a joint effort of the FBI and U.S. Customs disable his car and arrest him. He's led to an interrogation room where his former federal liaison Agent Bilkins informs him that they're looking to expose the drug lord Carter Verone, and they need his help to do it. Undercover U.S. Customs agent Monica Fuentes will set up a driving audition for Brian, using his criminal activity in order to make a more convincing case file, though also promising to wipe his record clean if he helps them.

Brian and Bilkins head to Barstow to attend a demolition derby race, won by none other than Brian's childhood friend, Roman Pearce. The two former friends soon break into a full-on brawl, which Brian wins due to Roman's lack of fighting experience. Eventually, he's able to convince Roman to help him in his case against Verone, promising to expunge his criminal record as well. Their rocky friendship certainly experiences some growing pains, but they work well enough together to pass Verone's test to become his drivers.

After winning new cars in a street race so that they can get rid of the bugged law enforcement-sanctioned vehicles, they soon learn that Verone has a major Miami detective on the take, putting the entire undercover team in danger. This leads Brian and Roman to patch things up between them before risking their lives on the final mission, which will have them transporting Verone's drug money and likely being executed upon delivery. Instead, the Miami police give chase, Brian and Roman switch cars, and their street-racing allies flood the city streets with hundreds of cars, confusing all pursuers. The final chase results in Brian and Roman racing a Camaro off a dock, crashing it into Verone's escape boat, where they're able to rescue Monica and apprehend the drug lord.

Brian and Roman hand over (most of) the money to the authorities and, now that their records are clean, talk about opening up a garage together in Miami. However, Brian was recruited by the FBI soon after; he became involved with the investigation into Arturo Braga, a case which had Letty Ortiz as one of its undercover operatives. She contacted Brian proposing to infiltrate Braga's cartel in exchange for a pardon for Dom. Brian took it as a personal failure when Letty was reported dead, murdered by one of Braga's men after being exposed.

Fast & Furious - Continuing his investigation into Braga's operation and Letty's death led Brian to cross paths with Dom once again. Though Brian gets Dom at gunpoint, he's forced to let him go once again in order to save a potential informant. Rather than going after him directly, he meets with Mia (who's brought in for an FBI interrogation) and warns her to stay away from Dom before she gets caught up in his mess. Instead, she blames him for all the trouble that has befallen her family, which Brian admits to shouldering his share of blame.

Despite losing to Dom in a race to become a driver on Braga's crew, Brian uses his position to have driver Dwight Miller arrested; he then takes Miller's place on the team. Now on the same side once again, he and Dom begin to joke about their former lives and wonder if it will complicate what they have to do. Luckily, Brian uses his insider knowledge that Braga plans to have his drivers killed upon delivery to formulate a plan of escape. But despite their narrow escape and Brian assisting a wounded Dom, when Toretto finds out about Brian's knowledge of Letty's involvement with the FBI, he attacks his former friend. Brian breaks the news to Dom that Letty did it all for him.

While apprehending Braga in Mexico and racing to bring him back to the U.S., Brian is injured in a T-bone collision by one of Braga's men, Fenix Calderon. Despite being attacked by Fenix, Brian is able to hold his ankles long enough for Dom to ram the hitman with a Camaro, saving Brian and avenging Letty.

When Brian cannot convince Dom to flee before the authorities arrive, he spends his time appealing Dom's sentence and requesting clemency, but to no avail. Instead, he leads a team in an attempt to bust Dom free from the prison transport bus.

Fast Five - After successfully freeing Dom, Brian and Mia distance themselves from him and head to Rio, seeking sanctuary with Vince and his family. After some brief, initial tension, Vince clues them both into a plot to boost expensive cars from a passenger train outside the city. They take the job, but Brian's suspicions are raised when he notes that the cars are under the protection of the DEA. Of course, these suspicions prove valid and the team is forced to make a daring escape in the stolen vehicles. After a high-flying drive off a cliff into the water below and a brief stint as hostages of heist mastermind Hernan Reyes, Brian and Dom are able to reunite with Mia and her stolen Ford GT40.

Brian blames Vince for the betrayal, but Dom's loyalty keeps the accusation from going any further, at least until Vince is caught returning a high-end microchip to Reyes. Brian is able to discover a shipment schedule and the location of $100 million in laundered money on the chip before Reyes' men and the DSS agents converge on them. It's after their narrow escape that Brian learns he's soon to become a father, a fact they soon share with Dom. Together, they plan to steal Reyes' money and use it to start a new life as a family somewhere safe from U.S. extradition.

After a successful heist and escape that causes quite a bit of destruction to Rio, the team decides to divvy up the money and go their separate ways. Brian, Mia, Dom, and Elena retire to Spain, though Brian and Dom opt to settle their rivalry with one last race.

Fast & Furious 6 - As Mia is about to give birth to her son Jack, Brian and Dom vow to never return to their old lives. However, a year later sees them longing for a return to Los Angeles. It's then that Dom shares the information on Letty's presumed death with Brian, information given to him by Hobbs in exchange for help tracking down Owen Shaw. Since Brian believed Hobbs to be messing with Dom's head to gain his cooperation, Brian accompanies his friend to London on their mission. Despite his mistrust of Hobbs, Brian and the team agree to help the DSS prevent the Nightshade device from falling into criminal hands, as long as all of their own records are wiped clean.

While Dom and Hobbs go after Shaw, Brian and the rest of the team pursue the other criminals in Shaw's crew, dodging sniper fire and attempting to take out each of the skilled drivers. After Roman and Tej's cars are disabled by electronic devices, Brian remains in sole pursuit until his own car is launched by a modified ramp car, causing Brian to crash. Though Shaw's team escapes with information on the location of Nightshade's final component, Brian is at least able to deduce the location of a pawn shop where Letty bought a gun used to shoot Dom at their most recent meeting.

When Brian learns that Shaw is associated with Braga, Brian goes undercover again to get into the maximum security prison in Los Angeles where Braga is being held. It turns out that Shaw gave up Letty's involvement with the FBI partially in exchange for Braga's supply of drugs, guns, and women. Braga doesn't give Brian much more to go on, except to say that they wouldn't find Shaw unless he wanted to be found, but Brian beats the man and his henchmen soundly anyway before calling in his ticket out of the clink.

New intel soon leads the team to a military convoy where Shaw has taken control of a tank, among other military vehicles. Improvising, Brian is able to save Roman from being crushed beneath the treads and simultaneously uses Roman's ruined car as an anchor meant to stop the tank in its tracks. During a quiet moment after the successful mission, Brian attempts to apologize to Letty for getting her involved, but she admits the choice was entirely hers.

Unfortunately, Brian and the team soon discover that Shaw's people have kidnapped Mia and are holding her hostage. They track down the plane she's being held in and assault it, with Brian and Mia ultimately being able to escape by driving off the moving plane in an Alfa Romeo Giulietta. Brian's then able to shoot the plane's wing flap in order to keep it from taking off, and they then get far enough away from the crash and resulting explosion to watch events unfold from afar.

After having their records cleared, the gang returns to the Toretto house in Los Angeles.

Furious 7 - Following the events in London, Brian is struggling to come to grips with civilian life and fatherhood. He admits to missing the blood and bullets of their action-packed lives, so Mia keeps her second pregnancy a secret from him for the time being. But when a bomb meant to kill the entire Toretto/O'Conner family only destroys their house instead, Brian and Mia soon find themselves thrust back into a dangerous situation. After setting up a safehouse for Mia and Jack in the Dominican Republic, Brian searches for Deckard Shaw and promises to make this mission his last one ever.

Brian makes a similar promise to Roman at Han's funeral in Los Angeles, saying that there would be no more funerals for their crew after Deckard is put into the ground. He's soon strapped into a driver's seat of a car that parachutes down to a road in the Caucasus Mountains as part of Mr. Nobody's plan to rescue the talented hacker known as Ramsey. Brian manages to board some of the convoy's vehicles, dispatch the guards, and rescue Ramsey, shoving her to a place of relative safety on the hood of Dom's Charger. Brian's own escape is narrower still as he's only just able to grab the tail end of Letty's Challenger as she drifts its back end out over the edge of a crumbling cliff.

After convincing Ramsey to share whatever information she knew with Dom's team, Brian confessed to Dom that he loved the adrenaline rush of their latest adventure. Though Dom admires his friend's courage, he says that the bravest thing he's done is being a good man to Mia and a father to Jack. However, both Brian and Dom show some bravery by driving a Lykan HyperSport through three luxury towers in order to keep the God's Eye surveillance device safely out of Deckard's hands. However, an ambush leaves Mr. Nobody injured and ends up with Deckard obtaining the God's Eye after all.

The team returns to Los Angeles where Brian calls Mia one last time, asking her to take care of Jack if he doesn't make it home. It's here that she reveals that they're expecting a baby girl; they reminisce about the way they met so many years ago. Brian then dons his FBI tactical gear for what will be his last mission, one way or another.

With Ramsey riding shotgun, he attempts to avoid a drone as long as possible before he's forced to hand her off into another car. Unable to shake the drone in the city, Brian dives out of his Nissan GT-R moments before the drone blows it up. He then runs to the nearest radio tower, dispatching henchmen along the way, in order to establish a network link-up so that Ramsey can finish hacking the God's Eye. As she works, Brian assists Hobbs in saving Dom from the wreckage of his crashed Charger. His attempts at CPR don't bring the unconscious Dom back, but Letty's words do.

After the threats are eliminated, Brian returns to spend quality time with his family. At a crossroads, Brian and Dominic reunite on the road before Brian diverges from Dom's path one last time.

The Fate of the Furious - Due to Walker's death and the crowd-pleasing farewell to Brian O'Conner in Furious 7, the character will not return. However, Dom's son has been named Brian in his honor.

Jordana Brewster as Mia Toretto

The Fast and the Furious - Though she's often resigned to being written as a love interest, damsel in distress, or "weakness" used as a bargaining chip by the bad guys, Jordana Brewster brings heat and heart to the portrayal of Mia Toretto. The character needed a strong personality in order to stand up to the likes of her brother Dom and to hold her own amidst his rough-and-tumble friends. Though Mia doesn't often get involved in the high-octane hijinks, she does have quite a few memorable moments.

Perhaps the richest role for Brewster was in creating Mia in the original 2001 film. Mia is no pushover; she keeps Dom in line, shuts down Vince's affections at every turn, and makes Brian earn every step forward in their relationship. In fact, their young romance crashes and burns when Brian reveals himself to be an undercover cop, which Mia takes to heart as an unforgivable betrayal that isn't resolved until a few films down the line. She's the heart and soul of this franchise, which has become more about family at its core than anyone would have guessed 15 years ago.

Fast & Furious - In this film, Mia is reduced to being a target of surveillance by the FBI, though she does have the important plot point of calling Dom to inform him of Letty's death. This carries on into the next film after the new allies are able to take Reyes down for good.

Fast & Furious 6 - Now in a relationship with Dom, they're living in the Canary Islands with Brian, Mia, and baby Jack. However, the news that Letty is alive drives a wedge between them; Elena takes the high road and gives Dom her blessing in searching for his wife, saying that she would do the same thing if she thought her husband was still alive. She plays a pivotal role in keeping Jack safe when Shaw's men come to kidnap Mia, who hands her baby over to Elena since she has come to trust her.

Though things don't work out for Dom and Elena in the end, she's happy that Dom and Letty are back together and respects his family-centric values. Elena then finds new employment alongside Hobbs with the DSS.

Furious 7 - However, this job doesn't last very long. Elena receives a letter of recommendation from Hobbs at the beginning of this film, though it's not long before an infiltration by Deckard Shaw leaves both Elena and Hobbs shaken. They're thrown through a window by an explosion which leaves Hobbs with a broken arm and leg. Elena then contacts Dom at the DSS's request and keeps an eye on Hobbs at the hospital, but other than that, she doesn't get in on much action.

The Fate of the Furious - Elena is back for this film, but in the harshest possible way. She's been taken hostage by Cipher, along with her son, whom she calls Marcos until his father, Dom, gives him a first name. Unfortunately, when Dom hesitates to follow Cipher's orders, the cyberterrorist's henchman Rhodes executes Elena. R.I.P.

Jason Statham as Deckard Shaw

Fast & Furious 6 - Rarely does a villain stick around in this franchise for more than one installment, but Jason Statham's Deckard Shaw has proven to be a worthy opponent for Dom and his crew. He was introduced to audiences at the tail end of the sixth film when he kills Han in order to avenge the severe injury to his younger brother, Owen. Shaw comes with a particular set of skills honed by his experience as an assassin in a clandestine organization and follows his own moral code. He earned positions as a lieutenant in the U.K.'s Special Boat Service and a major in the Special Air Service, but when his country no longer needed him, his superiors dispatched a team of agents to kill him. However, Deckard escaped and became a mercenary for hire.

Deckard ties into the rest of the franchise when it's revealed that he was the driver who T-boned Han's car and watched as the explosion killed him. He calls Dom to gloat over this fact and warns Toretto that he's coming for the rest of the team, which sets up the conflict in the next film.

Furious 7 - With Owen severely wounded after the events in London, Deckard takes out the security staff assigned to guard him and threatens the hospital staff with pain of death should they fail to adequately care for Owen. After thinking back on their childhood in which Deckard protected the younger Owen from the abuse of their father and other difficulties, he decides to do right by his little brother.

As Deckard aims to systematically take Dom's team apart, he first starts with an infiltration of the DSS to gain information on all of them. In doing so, he also injures Hobbs and nearly kills Elena. He follows that up by killing Han and sending a bomb to Toretto's house with the intent on wiping out a significant chunk of the team; he only succeeds in destroying their family home and royally pissing them off. Deckard then observes Han's funeral back in Los Angeles in order to lure Dom into a chase. His plan works, and after crashing their cars into each other in a game of chicken, Deckard gets the upper hand on Dom by pulling a gun on him.

Luckily for Dom, Mr. Nobody's forces step in and save his hide. Though Deckard retreats for the moment, he next attacks the team when they're attempting to rescue the hacker Ramsey from a mountaintop convoy. Though he fails in defeating them, he does form an alliance with the terrorist Mose Jakande. Dom and Brian are able to escape near death yet again when Deckard tracks them to a party in Abu Dhabi. They turn the tables on him and use the powerful surveillance program God's Eye to track Deckard down, but he's actually lying in wait with Jakande's mercenaries as his backup. Dom's crew escapes, but Deckard uses the newly obtained God's Eye to track them to Los Angeles.

An all-out battle ensues through the city streets, culminating with another street fight between Dom and Deckard. The villain holds his own until Jakande double crosses him and destroys part of the parking garage they're battling on. When Dom stomps on a section of the roof, it collapses beneath Deckard's feet. He survives the fall and resulting collapse, at which point Hobbs takes him into custody and places him in a maximum security prison.

The Fate of the Furious - This movie could have done a lot of things with Deckard Shaw and his brother Owen, but what it chose to do is perhaps the best course to take. It turns out that Deckard and Owen were coerced into their criminal behavior in the same way Dom was, by Cipher. Deckard breaks out of the ultramax security prison along with Hobbs when Mr. Nobody recruits the both of them to track down the rogue Dom Toretto. They form an awkward friendship based on mutual respect and toughness, as bros do.

Deckard then becomes a very useful member of the team, helping to sort out Cipher's systems and figure out just where the villainess is heading next. He's on the team that tracks Dom to New York, but when he confronts him, Dom presumably shoots him dead.

But as is the case in these movies, Deckard's death was a ruse that allowed him to infiltrate Cipher's plane later on thanks to a winged flight suit and his brother Owen by his side. This was all arranged thanks to Dom and Magdalene Shaw (Helen Mirren), Deckard and Owen's mother. Deckard not only took out Cipher's security team, he also rescued Dom's son and returned him safely to his father, securing a place within the extended family. He did, however, lose Cipher in the process since she parachuted from the plane.

Don't expect any main story cast or characters to show up, though there are some fun cameos here. new to the party is Rob Delaney's CIA agent Loeb. He recruits Shaw to bring in his own sister, Hattie, who's in possession of a deadly virus that threatens the entire world. Spurred on by the Shaw matriarch Queenie, who's in prison but rather enjoying her leisure time there, Deckard opts to track down Hattie in order to keep her safe but ends up having to work with his rival, Hobbs.

Throughout the movie, we get some backstory on the Shaw siblings. As kids, they would perform a number of grifts and heists, all with musical artist names like "The Mick Jagger" and "The Keith Moon." (Let's ignore the fact that Statham and Kirby are more than 20 years apart in age and just pretend that they could have been kids at the same time.) Perhaps more important are the hints at the backstory between Deckard and Brixton, the cybernetically enhanced super soldier who was once (nearly) executed by Deckard himself. While serving MI6, it was actually Brixton who betrayed the team and turned against his country with the promise of greater rewards through Eteon, perhaps connected through Cipher's cyberterrorist organization. This betrayal forces Deckard to go on the run as a mercenary for hire, but it's a breezy explanation for things like the murder of Han Seoul-Oh.

With an assist from Air Marshal Dinkley (Kevin Hart), the Shaws and Hobbs travel to Samoa to set up an ambush on Brixton, extract the virus from Hattie, and defeat the villainous organization of Eteon, temporarily. The mysterious leader of that organization wants to recruit both Hobbs and Shaw into their ranks. The men refuse, but they'll surely be dealing with them in the near future.

Having learned of Deckard's setup by Brixton, Hattie opts to reconnect with her sibling; together, they visit Queenie in prison and plan to help her escape. Together with Hobbs, Locke, and Loeb, this group is expected to return in the follow-up to Hobbs & Shaw.

Kurt Russell as Mr. Nobody

Furious 7 - If you're going to introduce a character who's as enigmatic and well-connected as Mr. Nobody this late in the game, you better have someone of Kurt Russell's veteran status to pull it off. Luckily, few people question Mr. Nobody's motives and nick-of-time arrivals because of the gravitas brought to the character by Russell, so it frees up the writers of the story to use a little deus ex machina when needed. In fact, Mr. Nobody's very introduction is the textbook definition of the term.

At the request of Hobbs and the DSS, covert government agent Mr. Nobody and his soldiers arrive on the scene to prevent Deckard Shaw from killing Dom. After a brief misunderstanding in which Dom almost chokes out Mr. Nobody's second in command, Sheppard, the two sides come to an agreement: Dom's crew will help rescue Ramsey for her knowledge about the God's Eye device, which they can then use to track down Deckard and bring him to justice.

However, the attempted apprehension of Deckard was a trap set by the villain, who ambushes Mr. Nobody, Sheppard, Dom, and Brian with the help of Jakande's men. Sheppard is killed and Mr. Nobody is injured, so Dom and Brian manage to help him escape to a safe distance. They leave him in the middle of the desert on the side of the road when he assures them that his medical team is en route.

The Fate of the Furious - Mr. Nobody returns, now with Scott Eastwood who appears as his protege, whom the team calls Little Nobody. Mr. Nobody's as slick as ever though his apprentice has a lot to learn. Luckily, Little Nobody learns quickly after getting on the wrong side of Hobbs, Roman, and the team, and becomes an asset to them by the movie's end. The Nobodies keep to the shadows for most of the movie, but they also help to pull the strings to get our heroes out of a tight spot now and then.

Eva Mendes as Monica Fuentes

2 Fast 2 Furious - Introduced as a protagonist in the franchise's second film, Eva Mendes' undercover U.S. Customs agent Monica Fuentes has returned over the years to play an integral role in some of the biggest moments. In her role as an assistant and lover to drug dealer Carter Verone, Monica comes into contact with Brian and Roman when they, too, go undercover to bring him down. Monica and Brian engage in a brief romance though Roman never trusts her completely. Unfortunately, her relationship with Brian causes her to blow her cover and put her own life at risk when she warns him that Verone plans to kill them after their drug delivery.

Brian and Roman come to the rescue, crashing a Camaro onto Verone's escape boat. This allows her to take Verone into custody at gunpoint, earning Roman's trust and impressing Brian a bit more for good measure.

Fast Five - Monica surfaces again a few movies later in this film's post-credits scene. She seen delivering a case file to Hobbs, one that has photographic evidence suggesting that Letty Ortiz is still alive.

The Fate of the Furious - Monica doesn't appear in this film.

Lucas Black as Sean Boswell

The Fast and the Furious: Tokyo Drift - When neither Brian nor Dom showed up in any serious capacity in the third installment in the franchise, that could have easily been the end of the whole Fast and Furious fever. However, Lucas Black's teenaged drift racer Sean Boswell has found an interesting niche within the fandom and was later worked into the overall plot structure rather than simply being kicked to the curb. The hothead and trouble-maker with a gift for street racing and a knack for auto mechanics is a familiar character in this franchise, but Sean provides an added, exotic wrinkle to the whole thing.

Before he ever ends up in Tokyo, Sean crashes his car during a drag race, one that he entered to impress a girl and save his pride. While everyone survives the wreck, Sean is arrested, only to be bailed out by his mother. Rather than risking continued bad behavior in Alabama, she sends him to live with his father, a U.S. Naval officer in Tokyo. If he's caught racing there, he'll go to jail; end of story.

Too bad Sean gets caught up with the drift racing scene in the city thanks to crossing paths with Takashi, the reigning Drift King and nephew of a Yakuza member, and Han, a street-racing legend. Sean may know racing, but he doesn't know how to drift, at least until Han offers to teach him. With Sean as Han's new errand boy, he starts living in his garage as he polishes his drifting skills and starts to get closer to Neela, Takashi's girlfriend. Unfortunately, Han's shady practice of stealing money from his business partners lands all of them in hot water; Takashi and his friends soon give chase to Han, Sean, Neela, and Sean's friend Twinkie. During the chase, they crash, but Han intervenes to keep them safe. It's here that Han is T-boned by Deckard Shaw, which results in a fiery death when the car explodes.

Since there was nothing they could do to save Han, Sean races back home to get help from his father, but Takashi threatens to kill both of them. Neela calms things by going with Takashi instead. Sean plans to talk to Takashi's uncle, a true Yakuza, return the money Han stole, and try to settle their differences in a final drift race. Sean is able to win against all odds by using the engine from Han's car installed in his father's 1967 Ford Mustang Fastback. However, the new Drift King already has an admirer, and someone who considers Han friendly wants to meet with him... Sean meets with Dominic Toretto, who's driving a car he won from Han in a race years ago. The two bond over a shared love of American muscle and the loss of Han, sealing their new friendship with a race.

Furious 7 - After their race, and after Sean admits to respecting Dom's speed and surprising drifting ability, Sean hands him items from Han's wreck: the picture of Gisele and Dom's cross necklace. Dom then implies that he's going to avenge Han's death by doing unspeakable things to his killer.

The Fate of the Furious - Characters have a habit of popping up throughout the Fast and Furious franchise, but scheduling conflicts with NCIS: New Orleans complicated this. Still, Sean might show up for the ninth and tenth films in the continuing franchise.

Nathalie Emmanuel as Ramsey

Furious 7 - One of the latest introductions to the franchise is the hacker Ramsey, played by up-and-comer Nathalie Emmanuel. The creator of the God's Eye technology-a device that can hack any camera and locate anyone, anywhere-Ramsey was aware of the power that her creation potentially held. For safe keeping, she sent it to her friend Safar in Abu Dhabi, but was kidnapped by the terrorist Mose Jakande who planned to interrogate her for information on the God's Eye.

However, Ramsey was rescued by Dom and his crew during their airborne assault on the Caucasus Mountains convoy, though she was nearly killed by Deckard Shaw in the process. Roman Pearce intervenes and sends Deckard off course, and Dominic crashes his car with Ramsey inside it in order to keep them out of the hands of Jakande. When they miraculously survive, Ramsey is cared for back at Dom's headquarters where they check to be sure she's okay. Having earned her trust, she confesses the location of the God's Eye device in Abu Dhabi.

While the team plans to crash the prince's party in order to recover the device, Ramsey helps Tej and Letty hack the luxury tower's security system and give Brian and Dom access to the ultra-rare car, the Lykan HyperSport. Once they regroup back at Dom's garage, Ramsey uses her creation to locate Deckard Shaw; unfortunately, the location is the site of an ambush on the part of Shaw and Jakande, who reclaims the God's Eye.

Back in Los Angeles, Ramsey attempts to hack her own device, but the crew is plagued by gunfire from airborne drones and a military helicopter. She's passed around from car to car to keep the device from locking in on her, buying her enough time to hack it. Thanks to Hobbs taking out the drone, she's finally able to access the God's Eye. She survives the final assault from Jakande and his military helicopter, and reconvenes with her new team on the beach as they watch Brian and Mia play with Jack.

It's Ramsey who gets to ask Dom if he plans on saying goodbye to them, to which Dom replies, "It's never goodbye."

The Fate of the Furious - Ramsey returns to help sort our the tech/hacker side of things. Most of her screentime includes technobabble alongside Tej and even Deckard as they attempt to track down Cipher and Dom. Ramsey does, however, question Dom's loyalty to the team, which puts her on Letty's shit list, though nothing comes of this. A "hacker battle" between Ramsey and Cipher buys the team some time when the cyberterrorist attempts to hijack the Russian sub and its nukes, but ultimately it comes down to horsepower and firepower.

Ramsey does get a brief love triangle tease, however, with herself, Roman and Tej. It's unclear if she'll end up with either one of them or not, but they're all together at the party.

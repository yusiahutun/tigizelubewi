A Georgia man was arrested Thursday on suspicion of murdering his ex-wife after months of harassment, bringing to a close a nearly weeklong manhunt.

John Kristofak, 58, was taken into custody in a Union City motel after a brief struggle with members of the Southeast Regional Fugitive Task Force.

According to police, officers recovered a gun at the scene, the Atlanta Journal Constitution reported.

Investigators say Kristofak killed his ex-wife, 48-year-old Donna Nations Kristofak, last Saturday after stalking her.

The woman was found unresponsive inside her east Cobb rental home. She was taken to WellStar Kennestone Hospital, where she later died.

In March, the 58-year-old man was arrested and charged with stalking Nations Kristofak. He pleaded guilty to the charge in October and was sentenced to seven months time served and five years’ probation.

The chain of events that led to Kristofak's capture started on at around noon last Saturday when Cobb police received a reported about a woman who was found unresponsive inside her home at 3960 Tall Pine Drive in the tony section of east Cobb.

Officers who arrived on the scene discovered Donna Nations Kristofak with serious injuries. She was rushed to a nearby hospital, where she passed away shortly after.

Police quickly secured a warrant for the arrest of John Kristofak on murder and aggravated assault charges in connection to the slaying of his wife of 20 years and the mother of their two sons.

On Monday, while officials were scouring the state in search of Kristofak, the Marietta Daily Journal received an email believed to be sent by the suspect killer in which he made suicidal threats.

'Let me say by the time you get this (I) will be dead,' the first line of the rambling missive stated.

In his letter filled with grammatical errors, the 58-year-old fugitive referring to himself in third person explained that about a year ago, he and his wife had been through a bitter divorce which was hard an everybody, especially their children.

'There is no justifying what I did and am sure I will burn in hell for it,' Kristofak wrote.

The man went on to say that he needed up behind bars for several months earlier this year when he violated a temporary order of protection which his ex-wife had obtained following a shoving match with him.

The 58-year-old man was then charged with aggravated stalking after he planted 'ugly signs' in Donna's yard.

He pleaded guilty to aggravated stalking and family violence simple battery charged on October 12, and was sentenced to four years and five months’ probation in addition to seven months in jail with credit for time served.

Kristofak was also issued a permanent restraining order banning him from going within 500 yards of his ex-wife or otherwise trying to contact her.

The husband explained in his communique that he blamed his wife and her neighbors of conspiring against him, adding that he felt depressed and angry, and should have sought help.

While police have not revealed how Donna Nations Kristofak was killed, the couple's teenaged said reportedly told family friends that his father stabbed his mother once in the torso after trapping her in the garage.

Following his arrest at Motel 6 Thursday morning, Kristofak was sent to the Cobb County Adult Detention Center, where he is being held without bond.

Family friend Laure Marks described the Kristofaks as a 'great couple.'

'We had them and their two sons over numerous times. We don't know what happened,' she told 11Alive.

Marks' son, Shane, who is friends with the Kristofaks' older son, reached out to him after the murder.

'My friend was actually home asleep. He said he heard his mom screaming and ran down to the garage.  That's when he saw his dad stabbing his mom,' said Shane Marks.

According to court records, Donna Nations Kristofak filed for divorce in August 2011 after 19 years of marriage and asked to get her maiden name back, Donna Jean Nations.

For years, the couple's marriage appeared to be a happy one, at least on the outside. The family lived in a tony neighbourhood in a home valued at nearly $500,000, and John Kristofak ran a cell phone business and later a mortgage firm.

However, trouble appeared on the horizon in 2007 when the Kristofaks filed for bankruptcy, saying that they have three mortgages on their home and an income averaging less than $20,000 a year.

From then point on, the relationship started to unravel at a rapid pace. Following their divorce, Donna Nations got a restraining order against her former spouse, who was under court supervision after being arrested on drug charges.

Around that time, the man reported warned Nations: 'you are in danger – protect yourself. No one can help you at this point.'

In early March, while he was supposed to be serving time, Kristofak had allegedly begun harassing Donna by calling her workplace about a dozen times, according to his arrest warrant.

The document states that Kristofak also sent her an email on March 19 that read: 'you are in danger – protect yourself. No one can help you at this point. You won’t know where it is coming from.'

Four minutes later, another e-mail came in: 'I warned you never cut me off from my children. You did and you will pay. I am not living past tonight so I will do anything to get you back.'

Minutes later, another asked, 'Have you ever been hit by a car going 140 not knowing where it’s coming from?'

In another communique, Kristofak allegedly advised his wife to 'make your final goodbyes with your children.'

Shortly after, the man allegedly hung posters on a tree in front of Donna Nations' house accusing her of 'vile, vulgar sexual things,' the warrant stated.

In late March, the 58-year-old allegedly followed his wife to a Walmart parking lot, where he pulled his car in front of his ex-wife's in order to stop her from leaving.

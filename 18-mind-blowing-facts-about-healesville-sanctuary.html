Home to over 200 species of Australian wildlife

Healesville Sanctuary is a haven for native Australian animals, housing more than 200 different species. From kangaroos and koalas to emus and wombats, the sanctuary provides a diverse and immersive experience in the heart of the Yarra Valley.

Founded in 1934

Established in 1934, Healesville Sanctuary has a rich history of conservation and wildlife rehabilitation. It has consistently played a significant role in protecting and promoting the unique wildlife of Australia.

Operated by Zoos Victoria

Healesville Sanctuary is one of three zoos operated by Zoos Victoria, a non-profit organization dedicated to wildlife conservation, animal welfare, and education. The sanctuary acts as an important breeding and research center to support the organization’s mission.

Boasts the world’s largest collection of Australian nocturnal animals

Within the sanctuary’s renowned Australian Wildlife Health Centre lies the world’s largest collection of Australian nocturnal animals. Visitors can observe unique animals such as sugar gliders, quolls, and bilbies in special night-time enclosures.

Offers exciting animal encounters

Healesville Sanctuary offers visitors the opportunity to get up close and personal with their favorite Australian animals through a range of exhilarating animal encounters. From feeding kangaroos to cuddling koalas, these experiences create lasting memories for visitors of all ages.

Features a stunning bushland setting

Situated in the picturesque Yarra Valley, Healesville Sanctuary is nestled within a beautiful bushland setting. The natural surroundings provide the perfect backdrop for observing native wildlife in their natural habitat.

Home to the iconic Australian platypus

Healesville Sanctuary is one of the few places where visitors can witness the magnificent platypus in action. These unique creatures, known for their duck-like bills and beaver-like tails, can be observed swimming and playing in specially designed enclosures.

Offers educational programs for children and schools

Healesville Sanctuary is committed to educating the next generation about the importance of wildlife conservation. The sanctuary offers a range of educational programs for children and schools, including interactive presentations, animal handling sessions, and behind-the-scenes tours.

Has a strong focus on wildlife rehabilitation

Healesville Sanctuary plays a vital role in wildlife rehabilitation, providing a safe haven for injured and orphaned animals. The sanctuary’s dedicated team of experts work tirelessly to rehabilitate animals and release them back into their natural habitats.

Showcases the critically endangered Helmeted Honeyeater

The sanctuary is actively involved in the conservation efforts of the critically endangered Helmeted Honeyeater. Visitors can learn about the sanctuary’s breeding and release programs aimed at increasing the population of this unique bird.

Hosts engaging wildlife shows and presentations

Healesville Sanctuary offers various wildlife shows and presentations throughout the day, providing visitors with exciting and informative experiences. From bird of prey demonstrations to feeding sessions with dingoes, these shows offer a deeper understanding and appreciation of Australian wildlife.

Offers unique behind-the-scenes experiences

For those seeking a more immersive experience, Healesville Sanctuary offers behind-the-scenes encounters. Visitors can go behind the scenes to meet the dedicated keepers, learn about animal care, and even assist with feeding some of the sanctuary’s furry residents.

Provides opportunities for photography enthusiasts

With its beautiful natural surroundings and abundance of wildlife, Healesville Sanctuary is a paradise for photography enthusiasts. Capture stunning images of iconic Australian animals in their habitats and take home unforgettable snapshots of your visit.

Features a Wildlife Rehabilitation Centre

The Wildlife Rehabilitation Centre at Healesville Sanctuary provides essential care and treatment for injured animals. Visitors can learn about the rehabilitation process and gain insights into the challenges faced by the dedicated wildlife carers.

Has a dedicated team of passionate animal keepers

The well-being and care of the animals at Healesville Sanctuary are in the capable hands of a team of passionate animal keepers. These professionals work tirelessly to ensure the health and happiness of the sanctuary’s diverse range of Australian wildlife.

Acts as a leading center for conservation research

Healesville Sanctuary is actively involved in cutting-edge conservation research, collaborating with other organizations to protect and preserve Australia’s unique wildlife. The sanctuary’s research helps inform conservation practices and contributes to the long-term survival of many native species.

Offers special events and experiences throughout the year

Throughout the year, Healesville Sanctuary hosts a range of special events and experiences to delight visitors. From Easter egg hunts to native plant sales, there is always something exciting happening at the sanctuary.

Provides opportunities for volunteering

Healesville Sanctuary offers opportunities for volunteering, allowing individuals to actively contribute to the conservation and care of Australian wildlife. Volunteering at the sanctuary is a rewarding experience that enables people to make a meaningful difference.

FAQs

1. What are the opening hours of Healesville Sanctuary?
Healesville Sanctuary is open from 9:00 AM to 5:00 PM every day of the year, including public holidays.

Yes, guided tours are available at Healesville Sanctuary. Visitors have the option to join a Koala Encounter, Spirits of the Sky presentation, or a behind-the-scenes tour to learn more about the incredible wildlife at the sanctuary.

7. Is there parking available at Healesville Sanctuary?
Yes, there is ample parking available at Healesville Sanctuary. Visitors can park their vehicles free of charge.

Uhuru, Ruto Hang out at State House with Their Wives after Jamhuri Day Celebrations

 Posted on August 7, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  431 words  |  Aldo Pusey
  • After Jamhuri Day celebrations, President Uhuru Kenyatta and his deputy spent time together in State House Nairobi
  • The two, who were in the company of their wives, were all smiles as they posed for the camera together
  • The two top leaders had earlier, during the fete, got Kenyans talking after sharing hearty laughter together

President Uhuru Kenyatta and his estranged deputy William Ruto took time to hang out at State House Nairobi after the Jamhuri Day celebrations on Sunday, December 12.

Uhuru, Ruto differences aside

The two top leaders put aside their differences on Sunday and put up a united front as they led the country to mark the 58th anniversary of Kenya's full independence at Uhuru Gardens.

In pictures shared by the second lady Rachel Ruto, the two political buddies turned foes also spent the afternoon at state house together with their spouses.

The first families even took a moment to take pictures together and they were all smiles.

"Privileged to enjoy the splendid hospitality of our gracious First Lady, Her Excellency Margaret Kenyatta at State House, Nairobi on Jamhuri Day," Rachel wrote.

In a video sighted by TUKO.co.ke, Uhuru and Ruto were also spotted enjoying the music from a choir at state house.

Hearty laughter

During the Jamhuri Day celebrations at Uhuru Gardens, the two politicians who have been reading from different political scripts got Kenyans talking after sharing hearty laughter.

After his arrival at the stadium in his full military regalia, the head of state saluted Ruto before greeting him.

The two were captured sharing a hearty laugh during the heartwarming moment that led many to believe that despite their political differences, there still lay an ounce of chemistry between them.

Uhuru invites Raila to speak

Just before concluding his speech, the president momentarily stopped his speech and invited his handshake buddy Raila Odinga to address Kenyans.

While praising the ODM leader for work well done at Uhuru Gardens, the audience could not help but cheer upon hearing his name forcing Uhuru to invite him to address the nation.

"I want to thank you Raila Odinga for helping us. Looks like these people want to hear from you. Come say hi," he said.

In his speech, Raila in turn congratulated Uhuru for saving Uhuru Gardens, from who he termed as landgrabbers.

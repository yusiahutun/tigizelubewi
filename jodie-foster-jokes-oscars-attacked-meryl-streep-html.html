Jodie Foster jokes during Oscars she was attacked by Meryl Streep

 Posted on August 7, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  584 words  |  Martina Birk

Jodie Foster didn't let an injury stop her from presenting at the 90th Academy Awards from the Dolby Theatre in Los Angeles on Sunday.

The 55-year-old actress-director emerged on crutches to co-present the honors for Best Actress alongside fellow A-list actress Jennifer Lawrence, and had a topical comeback when Lawrence asked her about the crutches, referencing colleague Meryl Streep.

'Streep - she I, Tonya-ed me,' said Foster, in reference to the lauded feature film documenting Tonya Harding's 1994 plot to cripple her figure skating rival Nancy Kerrigan.

Lawrence was in on the joke, chiming in, 'No, it's cool - you know, she tripped me once!,' before remarking that Streep 'was always nice at the luncheons.'

Foster responded, 'Eh, she’s acting.'

Foster was actually injured weeks back when she fell while skiing, a spokesperson for the star told People. Foster wore a white blazer with black pants and shoes all by Ralph Lauren Collection to the event, with her hair down and parted; while J-Law was clad in a resplendent glittering shoulder-less golden gown with her locks curled and parted.

Foster, a double Oscar-winner for her roles in 1991's The Silence of the Lambs and 1988's The Accused - and Lawrence, who won Best Actress for her role in 2012's Silver Linings Playbook - were substitute presenters for last year's Best Actor winner Casey Affleck.

Resilient: The double Oscar-winner injured herself skiing, her rep told The Hollywood Reporter

 Elegant: Foster wore a white blazer with black pants and shoes by Ralph Lauren Collection to the event, while J-Law was clad in a resplendent glittering golden gown

Sense of humor: Jodie suggested that her show business colleague Meryl Streep (R) pulled Tonya Harding antics on her

 Pointed: Foster mockingly pointed at Streep during the joke she told 

Push-back: Foster and Lawrence were substitutes for Casey Affleck, who bowed out amid the controversy of sexual harassment allegations that were raised against him in lawsuits he later settled 

Affleck, who took home the award last year for Manchester by the Sea, bowed out of the spot in January amid the rise of the #MeToo movement, as his presence last year was a source of contention amid settlements he made in response to sexual harassment lawsuits filed in 2010 over his alleged behavior during production of the movie I’m Still Here.

Upon his withdrawal from his presenting duties, the Academy said in a statement, 'We appreciate the decision to keep the focus on the show and on the great work of this year.'

Foster and Lawrence would up presenting the statue to Frances McDormand for her role in Three Billboards Outside Ebbing, Missouri. 

Dynamic duo: The mega-stars previously worked together on the 2011 movie The Beaver, which Jodie directed and starred in

Fresh: J-Law declared, 'It's a new day in Hollywood,' calling Foster an inspiration to her

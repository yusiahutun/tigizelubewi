Hamptons schoolgirl, 18, dies after falling 200ft on outdoor activities hiking trip in Colorado

A teenage girl died after falling down a 200ft canyon during a university hiking trip in Colorado.

Caitlin Kremp had broken off from her group to climb while watching the sunrise when she fell and her body was found by a search and rescue crew on Tuesday morning.

The 18-year-old has been hailed as ‘the perfect inspiration’ by friends after her death at the Chicago Basin area, part of the San Juan National Forest.

Caitlin Kremp, 18, died while on a hiking trip with her fellow students from Virginia Commonwealth University

As friends and family gathered to mourn the Hampton teen, they described the straight A student as an outdoorsy athlete who loved life and lived it to the fullest.

‘She was that perfect inspiration,’ said Esmeralda Coes, one of Kremp's former teammates form her high school field hockey team.

Caitlin Kremp died doing what she loved

‘She was that person if you felt the need to talk to someone, she was always there.’

Kremp, Coes and their team at Phoebus High School appeared on MTV’s show Made where viewers saw Caitlin take on a main role which her former coach put down to her being ‘a natural leader’.

Speaking to local news channel WavyTv, Ryan Pringle said: ‘Caitlin had immense potential, a lot of potential.

‘She had the right attitude. She had the right outlook on life.’

Among the mourners is Olympian Keli Smith-Puzo who coached Caitlin Kremp and her high school hockey team during the Made episode.

The Olympic women's field hockey team player tweeted: ‘Friday, I'll play my final game in the Olympics in honor of Caitlin Kremp, an amazing young woman that I was lucky to know! R.I.P. Caitlin.’

Caitlin Kemp's high school friend Esmeralda Coes called her the 'perfect inspiration'

Officials from the Victoria Commonwealth University, where Caitlin studied Business and Environmental studies, said the teenager had been among a group of students from the university’s Recreational Sports Department.

The trip, sponsored by the Outdoor Adventure Program where Kremp was a student worker, consisted of a four day hike during which the accident occurred.

The La Plata County Sheriff’s Department said in a statement that emergency services received a distress call shortly after 8am and were told of Miss Kremp’s death upon arrival.

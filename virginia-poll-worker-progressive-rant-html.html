Foul-mouthed progressive uses f-word THIRTEEN times during 90-second rant at GOP poll greeter in Vir

 Posted on August 15, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  637 words  |  Larita Shotwell

A progressive was filmed during a profanity-filled rant against a Republican in Virginia who he accused of being a 'KKK-supporting, Bible-beating bigot freak.'

Matthew Hurtt, who chairs the Arlington GOP, encountered the unidentified man on Tuesday at a polling location, where he was handing out Republican sample ballots during the election.

The GOP operative said the man actually verbally abused him twice - once on the way inside the polling center, and again when he left, which is the 90-second rant Hurtt captured on camera. A camera captured the ranter saying the f-word a total of 13 times during the brief interaction. 

'You f****** people tried to overturn the election! You might as well have been walking up to my head at the polling station and putting a gun to my head trying to tell me not to vote, and you expect me to take that f****** personally?' the man began. 'You try to overturn elections with violence and then you're out here, among decent people..'

Hurtt then thanks the man for 'coming out today,' which seems to only make him angrier.

The man adds: 'What's on your f******... what are your policy prescriptions? f****** rapist's rights? involving yourself in people's fucking families? F****** bedrooms? You f****** animal.'

Things then get even uglier as Hurtt tells the man to 'have a nice day.'

'You try to steal my vote next year, I'm going to f******remember you personally. In fact, I'm telling a f****** picture of you. Is that alright? sh** bird?' the man continues.

When Hurt tells the man that's fine because Virginia only requires one-party consent for recording, the man says: 'Yeah a one-party state - you want a fucking one-party country, is what you want.

Hurtt then turns his attention to another voter, but the man continues the rant, this time addressing the voter.

'You know, try not to be buddy-buddy with these people cause they put on the face of a good neighbor, but they support lynch mobs and the f***** KKK, or they're f**** Bible-beating bigots and freaks' he tells the voter.

The clip has gone viral on X, formerly known as Twitter, with many showing support for Hurtt and demanding the man's identity be exposed.

Hurtt elaborated on the incident and said: 'I had already talked to the Democratic poll greeters and a few voters when this man approached me,' Hurtt told the Washington Examiner. 

'I offered a Republican sample ballot, and he flew into a rage, calling me names, spouting the sort of tropes you hear in the video. Then he went inside to vote.

'I assumed he would come out afterwards and continue his verbal assault, so I turned on my phone camera and slid it into my shirt pocket. What you see in the video is actually the second exchange.' 

Although his name won’t appear on the ballot, Republican Governor Glenn Youngkin looms large over Tuesday’s general election in Virginia.

Republicans hope to gain full control of the state legislature and clear a path for the governor to enact his proposals on abortion, education, taxes and other key policy priorities. 

Democrats seek to maintain control of at least one chamber and possibly flip the other to continue to serve as a check against Youngkin’s agenda.

In 2021, Republicans won a narrow 52-48 majority in the House of Delegates and swept every statewide constitutional office. Meanwhile, Democrats hold a slim 22-17 majority in the state Senate. An additional state Senate seat most recently held by a Republican is vacant.

William Gao On Heartstopper

William played “Tao Xu,” one of Charlie’s best friends on Heartstopper. The other two included Elle (Yasmin Finney) and Isaac (Tobie Donovan).

This was the first time William ever appeared on the small screen. But being there definitely wasn’t easy. According to the actor, he had to go through four rounds of an open audition before he finally landed the role.

Joining William for the inaugural season were its casts — Joe Locke (Charlie Spring), Kit Connor (Nick Nelson), Yasmin Finney (Elle Argent), Sebastian Croft (Ben Hope), Corinna Brown (Tara Jones), Kizzy Edgell (Darcy Olsson), Jenny Walser (Tori Spring), Cormac Hyde-Corrin (Harry Greene), Sebastian Croft (Ben Hope), Echo (Nellie), Fisayo Akinade (Mr. Ajayi), and Chetna Pandya (Coach Singh).

Also, there were two new characters, Isaac Henderson (Tobie Donovan) and Imogen Heaney (Rhea Norwood), added to the cast who are mentioned in the graphic novels.

For those who haven’t seen Heartstopper, it revolves around Charlie and his sports-loving crush Nick who discover their unlikely friendship might be something more as they navigate school and young love.

Besides acting, William is also blessed with a talent for music. He was a member of The Wasia Project and played the keys for the band. Some of their popular Eps and singles available on Spotify were Why Don’t U Love Me, U Deserve, Burning Eyes R Calling, and Misfit Biscuit.

As for his music inspirations, William is a fan of the Beatles, and Guys and Dolls.

Is William Gao Gay?

Though William Gao might appear on Heartstopper — a drama to inspire a whole new generation of LGBTQ+ youth — there was zero evidence that he was gay IRL. Moreover, even William’s character wasn’t gay on the show.

The show mainly focused on the love story between the openly gay overthinker Charlie and rugby player Nick. It featured the elements of bullying and of sexual assault in a minuscule part of a really happy story.

Overall, the show was a good way to introduce kids to the concept of being queer. “It’s not like there’s a video that you can put on and show kids to teach them how to be towards queer people, or if they are queer, to figure it out. There are not many kid-friendly ways to do that so I think it’s helpful,” actress Kizzy Edgell explained.

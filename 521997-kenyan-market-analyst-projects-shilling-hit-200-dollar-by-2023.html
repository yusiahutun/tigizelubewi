Kenyan Market Analyst Projects Shilling Will Hit 200 Against Dollar by End of 2023

 Posted on April 17, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  570 words  |  Larita Shotwell
  • The shilling has lost 19% of its value against the US dollar since the start of the year, pushing up the cost of imports
  • On Thursday, September 21, the Central Bank of Kenya quoted the shilling at 147.3 against the US currency
  • FX Pesa lead market analyst Rufas Kamau projected the shilling would continue to slide after the US Fed retained high interest rates

The Kenyan shilling's freefall against the dollar is expected to continue, hurting the economy and increasing the cost of imports.

On Thursday, September 21, the Central Bank of Kenya quoted the shilling at 147.3 against the dollar.

The shilling has lost 19% of its value against the US currency since the start of the year.

Why has the Kenyan shilling depreciated?

In an exclusive interview with TUKO.co.ke, FX Pesa lead market analyst Rufas Kamau explained that the Kenyan shilling has been experiencing a steady decline against the US dollar.

Kamau noted that the Kenyan currency registered an average monthly depreciation rate of 2.16% in 2023 due to high fuel prices and a strong dollar.

"If this trend continues, the exchange rate is anticipated to reach 160.45 by the end of the year. However, the Kenyan shilling faces significant challenges in the form of surging crude oil prices and the strengthening US dollar," the analyst expounded.

Why shilling could hit 200 against dollar

The United States (US) Federal Open Market Committee said interest rates will stay higher for longer.

US Federal Reserve expects to keep interest rates over 5% through 2024, making short-term US bonds quite appealing to investors.

"Considering the current US inflation rate of 3.7%, an investor purchasing 1-year US treasuries can expect a return of 5.487%. From a Kenyan perspective, this translates to an attractive return of 25.5%, surpassing the yields offered by Kenyan bonds.

This substantial difference in returns is expected to lead investors to shift their focus away from Kenyan bonds in pursuit of higher yields in currencies that are not experiencing rapid depreciation. Consequently, I project that the Kenyan shilling is likely to reach a level of 200 against the US dollar by the end of the year," Kamau explained.

What are implications of weak shilling?

Kamau predicted the cost of living will soar to an unprecedented level if the shilling continues to slide.

In this case, employees will begin to demand higher salaries, while employers will start hiking prices to accommodate higher salaries, leading to a wage-price spiral.

While the high rate against the dollar supports exports by making Kenyan goods cheaper to the rest of the world, it makes imports more expensive, putting more strain on household budgets.

Dollar shortage

Most commercial banks increased the rate of selling the dollar to over KSh 150 as the shortage of foreign currency increased.

The hike could lead to a new wave of price increases as traders and business people are forced to dig deeper into their pockets to pay for imports.

Kenyans Slam Education CS Ezekiel Machogu for Postponing School Opening at Night: &quot;Should Apologise&quot;

 Posted on June 16, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  558 words  |  Larita Shotwell
  • On Sunday, April 28, Education Cabinet Secretary Ezekiel Machogu pushed the opening of schools by a week
  • However, many Kenyans have expressed disappointment with the Education Cabinet Secretary's decision
  • Kenyans questioned why Machogu sent out the statement late at night when some parents had already sent their children to school.

Amos Khaemba, a journalist at TUKO.co.ke, brings more than three years of experience covering politics and current affairs in Kenya.

Nairobi - Kenyans have criticised the decision by Education Cabinet Secretary Ezekiel Machogu to postpone the opening of public schools.

Why did CS Machogu postpone schools opening?

On Monday, April 29, Machogu pushed the opening dates just a few hours before schools opened because of the ongoing rains and floods that have claimed over 70 lives.

In a statement released by the Ministry of Education, the CS said the decision was arrived at after reports from field officers indicated many schools had been affected by floods.

"The Ministry of Education has resolved to postpone the reopening of all primary and secondary schools by one week, to Monday, May 6, 2024," Machogu said.

However, Kenyans questioned the decision by the Education Ministry to make public such information dead in the night.

They wondered why the Cabinet Secretary had to wait until 1.00am before making such a crucial decision.

Others lamented that some parents had already released their children to travel to school after the government insisted that the schools would open as earlier planned.

How Kenyans reacted to Machogu's decision

Ahmed:

"Exactly!!! I HAVE SO MANY QUESTIONS for CS @machoguezekiel. Many boarders had already travelled to their schools overnight so as to report this morning. Are they to be returned back home? Won’t that be an even bigger risk to the school children who’ve travelled, besides the costs? Should the schools keep them for a week?"

Meet Three FIT Students Taking the Fashion World by Storm

 Posted on October 4, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  500 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

They dreamed of seeing their clothes on the runway. And worn by people everywhere. And one night last month, three young Fashion Institute of Technology (FIT) students—Brandon Johnson, Kathleen Frey, and Sonne Bajwa—saw their careers take off at the Harper’s BAZAAR ICONS gala, held at the Plaza Hotel, as they celebrated winning the second annual Infor/FIT Fashion Design ICONS Awards.

Infor

Friend of a Friend editor-in-chief Olivia Perez wearing the winning piece.

“New York has always been a mecca in the fashion world. So as a design student, to be a part of that is amazing,” said Brandon Johnson, the 21-year-old Kansas native who won the design award with a showstopping piece inspired by his mother’s pearls. “I’m having an out-of-body experience thinking about it.”

“All my friends are going crazy,” said Sonne Bajwa, one-half of the duo that won the awards’ business contest. “My family’s going crazy.” Kathleen Frey, Bajwa’s co-winner would agree: “Being surrounded by all these people…the energy here is absolutely amazing.”

To say that the event was star-studded would be an understatement. Partygoers included Selena Gomez, The Weeknd, Gigi Hadid, Kim Kardashian West, Kaia Gerber, Ashley Graham, Joan Smalls, Karlie Kloss, Iris Apfel, Kendall Jenner, Heidi Klum, Courtney Love, and Carine Roitfeld—who put together the guest list.

Infor

Infor/FIT Fashion Design ICONS Award Panel Judges Marigay McKee, Durand Guion, and Temple St. Clair with Carine Roitfeld.

Infor, the leading provider of cloud applications for fashion and retail, teamed up with FIT and Hearst Magazines for the awards—a two-part competition that fosters collaboration between designers and the business teams that advance the creative vision, bringing designs to the closets of multitudes.

The contest was comprehensive. The first round was all about “concept to runway,” while the second focused on “garment to closet,” where contenders were challenged to successfully market the winning garment to the world. Johnson, the winner of the design portion of the competition, worked with couturier Paula Versalona to make his multi-piece dress leap from page to reality. Frey and Bajwa—the two FIT students in the International Trade and Marketing program who won the business award—crafted an ingenious multi-platform plan that addressed the task of selling 1,000 pieces of Johnson’s design via e-commerce and at 50 Macy’s stores, using technology such as Infor’s Customer Experience Suite, in addition to research and market data. They were judged on strategy, persuasiveness, presentation, and feasibility.

Infor

Friend of a Friend’s Olivia Perez with the winners of Infor/FIT Fashion Design ICONS Awards Brandon Johnson, Kathleen Frey, and Sonne Bajwa.

And at the gala—which was held on September 8—Johnson, Bajwa, and Frey saw everything they worked so hard for come to life: fashion influencer and Friend of a Friend editor-in-chief, Olivia Perez, wore the winning piece for all of the fashion world to see. “It’s so exciting to be here on his [Brandon’s] behalf and wear his incredible design,” said Perez.

It’s clear that these three FIT students went above and beyond to accomplish their aspirations. And this is just the beginning.

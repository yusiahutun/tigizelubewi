Full Moon in Aries Will Affect Each Zodiac Sign 2023

 Posted on July 20, 2024  |  9 minutes  |  1875 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Although it’s no secret that relationships are the key ingredient to Libra season, we more than often overlook the relationship we have with ourselves, or the lack thereof. Sounds contradicting when referring to the harmonious essence of this Venus-ruled season, but self-love is key when considering how the full moon in Aries of September 2023 will affect each zodiac sign. The astrology of this lunation is also a conundrum, so let’s break it down from the top.

First thing’s first, the full moon phase marks a period of culmination, completion, celebration, and heightened awareness. This occurs when the sun makes a direct opposition to the moon, which not only represents a call for balance—between the masculine (sun) and feminine (moon) energies within us—but also presents us with the opportunity to confront energies that have been hidden from our conscious minds. Similar to the way the moon illuminates the night sky, the full moon essentially puts a spotlight on our inner world, and emotional landscape. For reference, think back to the new moon in Aries that took place on March 21. What intentions did you have in mind, specifically when in regards to your individuality, independence, and self-autonomy?

On September 29 at exactly 5:57 a.m. ET, the moon will reach its peak of fullness via six degrees of Aries. Check which astrological house belongs to six degrees Aries in your birth chart, and the same goes for the sun in Libra! After all, while the sun sheds light on everything from your one-on-one partnerships and ability to compromise (Libra), the moon is bringing our red-hot passions and desire for self-autonomy (Aries) to the forefront. How has this affected your emotional state? What is driving you forward? Is it in alignment with your current commitments? If you’ve been contemplating whether to start anew, are you ready to share these ideas with your significant other(s)? Here’s where the full-moon energy gets tricky.

The ruler of this lunation is none other than go-getter Mars, the planet of aggression, combat, energy, leadership, sexuality, and war. Mars, however, happens to be transiting through harmony-seeking Libra, a zodiac sign where the red planet is uncomfortable, as it cannot express itself fully. In simpler terms, while Mars would normally inspire us to move forward with confidence and stamina, Mars in Libra could be the reason why we’re second-guessing our desires, and/or feeling the need to depend on the opinions of others. Moreover, Mars will be sitting alongside the South Node in Libra which, as you may know, represents what needs to be released at this time, both collectively and as individuals. 

Be mindful of the patterns we’re ready to release and make peace with, as there are energies (i.e. relationships, codependency, people-pleasing, fear of confrontation) surrounding us that don’t serve our highest good. Mars will also be making an opposition to Chiron—the “wounded healer”—triggering our vulnerabilities. However, instead of succumbing to self-sabotaging patterns, this is an opportunity for you to rise above the pain, and insecurity. Venus will also be making its third and final square to change-maker Uranus, which could inspire sudden and disruptive events, specifically in the realm of relationships, and finances. Fortunately, with the help of lucky Jupiter (retrograde alongside Uranus) these unexpected and/or unconventional plans are being supported by the cosmos.

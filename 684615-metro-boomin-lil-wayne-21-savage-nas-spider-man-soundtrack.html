Metro Boomin Adds Lil Wayne, Nas &amp; 2 Chainz To &quot;Spider-Man&quot; Soundtrack

 Posted on February 8, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  386 words  |  Martina Birk

Miles Morales returns this Friday with the release of Spider-Man: Across The Spider-Verse. However, it’s the film’s soundtrack that has many non-Marvel fans ecstatic. With Metro Boomin at the helm of the upcoming project, he’s undoubtedly brought in a number of his close collaborators and friends, many of which are already staples on our regular playlists. He previously confirmed James Blake, Don Toliver, and Future would appear on the project. However, he’s unveiled more names as we get closer to the soundtrack’s release date.

On Tuesday (May 30th), Metro Boomin confirmed a slew of other stars who will grace the project. His buddy Nav will appear on the tracklist, along with Coi Leray, Lil Uzi Vert, Offset, JID, Swae Lee, and more. He also tapped international stars like WizKid and Toian to flesh out the Spider-Man: Across The Spider-Verse tracklist. While many of the artists collaborating with Metro on the project are from his generation, he’s tapped a few legendary figures with decades under their belt to join him on the sonic experience.

Lil Wayne Features On Metro Boomin’s Spider-Man Soundtrack

Metro Boomin will bring on some of rap’s most revered lyricists for the upcoming Spider-Man: Across The Spider-Verse soundtrack. The St. Louis producer shared Weezy’s Spidersona on his Instagram post. “The best rapper alive Lil Wayne is confirmed for the @spiderversemovie soundtrack available everywhere June 2nd!!” he captioned the post. Afterward, he confirmed Metro also confirmed Nas as a feature on the forthcoming effort.

As impressive as the features are already, it wouldn’t be a Metro Boomin effort without a collaboration from 21 Savage, who he also confirmed would appear on the tracklist. Additionally, A$AP Rocky, who he apparently has loads of music with, will also appear on the project. We’ll also find Metro Boomin and 2 Chainz connect for Spider-Man: Across The Spider-Verse OST. At this rate, it seems like Metro won’t be releasing a single, leaving us waiting until Friday’s release date. 

Nas, 21 Savage & A$AP Rocky Confirmed

The star-studded tracklist will certainly have bangers to ride out to this summer. Even though it’s been a few months since Metro Boomin released Heroes & Villains, his upcoming soundtrack for Spider-Man: Across The Spider-Verse is already shaping up to be an incredible follow-up by the features alone.

Barcelona 'FINALLY tie up $4m deal for LA Galaxy right back Julian Araujo after late paperwork chaos

 Posted on March 12, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  287 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Mexican international right back Julian Araujo will finally be able to make his dream move to FC Barcelona after a deal was agreed between his future club and his current team - LA Galaxy.

Araujo was initially set to join the Catalan giants in the January transfer window, however his deal was waylaid by pending transfers and confirmation from MLS.

But now, according to Fabrizio Romano, Araujo is expected to travel to Barcelona to finally confirm a contract until June of 2026. The deal is expected to cost $4million. 


As a result, his paperwork was filed 18 seconds past the deadline and subsequent decisions from FIFA meant that Araujo would not be moving anywhere.

Per Mexican sports journalist Kery Ruiz, Barcelona delayed starting because 'they wanted to sell [Hector] Bellerín first.'

While that move eventually went through, it delayed the finer touches for Araujo's deal - which proved costly.

LA Galaxy right back Julian Araujo will finally get his move to Europe and to FC Barcelona

It appears Xavi will finally get his man after a mess-up with sending in papers killed the deal

A delay in Barca's deal to sell Hector Bellerin was partially responsible for the initial deal failing

According to ESPN, if Araujo's transfer had been sealed then the Mexican would've been registered to play for Barca's B team.

However, due to the club's current financial situation, it's possible that Barcelona would have had him play with the first team right away.

The 21-year-old has already played in 102 combined MLS matches, five US Open Cup matches, and two CONCACAF Leagues Cup matches.

He has two goals and 14 assists in his time with the Galaxy and their second team playing in the USL Championship.
